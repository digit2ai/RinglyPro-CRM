The Problem

Four challenges that cost deals and slow robot placements

Sales managers and directors struggle with inconsistent business plans, fragmented surgeon data, unquantified clinical outcomes, and zero proforma tracking.

1. Inconsistent Business Plans

Too much variability in how plans are built. Skill levels vary, it takes too long to learn, and the result is inaccurate ROI projections that erode credibility with hospital C-suites.

2. Surgeon Data Gaps

Incremental volume commitments from surgeons are gathered ad-hoc -- field conversations, emailed surveys, scattered spreadsheets. Data never flows directly into the business plan.

3. Clinical Outcomes Not Dollarized

da Vinci delivers superior clinical outcomes -- lower infections, shorter stays, fewer readmissions. But nobody is quantifying the dollar value and adding it to the ROI model.

4. No Proforma Tracking

After the robot is placed, there is no system to compare projected vs. actual surgeon volumes. Managers cannot validate the investment or demonstrate ongoing ROI to customers.
