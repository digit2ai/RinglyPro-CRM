2 What MCP Neural AI actually means

It's a coordinated team of AI agents — not one chatbot.

"MCP" stands for Model Context Protocol — the way our specialized agents share context and hand work between each other. When a customer brings us a problem, we don't throw it at one big model; we route it through agents that each do one thing very well.

Why this matters

One generic chatbot can't both research a market, draft a board deck, and write production code. Our agents are specialized and chained — each agent's output is the next agent's input. The customer gets work product that's actually usable, not a vague summary.

What the customer gets

Concrete deliverables on a calendar: a slide-by-slide deck, a working web app, a board memo, an AI agent that answers their phones — produced by the right agent for the job, reviewed by a human, and shipped on a defined timeline.
