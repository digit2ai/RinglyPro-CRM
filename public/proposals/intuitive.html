Sales Intelligence Platform

What Your Sales Team Gets — In Plain English

Instead of walking into a hospital with a generic brochure, your reps walk in with a data-driven assessment that says: "We analyzed YOUR hospital, YOUR procedures, YOUR financials — and here's exactly which robot you need, when it pays for itself, and how to grow from there." That's what closes $2M deals.

1
Hospital Profile — "Here's who you are"
Shows the hospital's basic facts at a glance: how big they are (beds), what kind of surgeries they do (urology 25%, general surgery 35%, etc.), how many surgeons they have, and whether they already have a robot. This is the "we did our homework" slide that builds credibility in the first 30 seconds.
2
Procedure Pareto (Gini Coefficient) — "Which surgeries should go robotic first?"
Out of all the surgeries this hospital does, which ones drive the most volume? The Gini coefficient is a single number (0 to 1) that tells you how concentrated the volume is. A high Gini (0.7+) means a small number of procedure types account for most cases — which is ideal for robotics because you target just those high-volume procedures and capture 80% of the opportunity. The ABC table ranks every procedure from A (automate first) to C (do later). This tells the sales rep exactly which surgeons to target and which procedures to lead with.
3
Monthly Seasonality — "When is the hospital busiest?"
Shows which months have the most surgeries. January is typically heavy (patients meet deductibles), December is slow (holidays). The CoV (coefficient of variation) tells you how predictable the volume is. Low CoV = steady volume = robot consistently utilized. High CoV = peaks and valleys = risk of the robot sitting idle some months. This directly impacts whether the hospital should buy or lease.
4
Weekday Distribution — "Which days are the ORs packed?"
Shows surgical volume by day of week. Tuesday and Wednesday are typically the busiest OR days. Saturday minimal, Sunday zero. This reveals scheduling bottlenecks — if Tuesday is maxed out, that's where a second system is justified. It also tells the rep: "Your robot runs hard Mon-Fri, sits idle weekends."
5
Hourly OR Utilization — "When do the ORs peak during the day?"
Hour-by-hour pattern: first case at 7am, peak 8am-noon, taper after 3pm. Critical for system capacity planning — the robot needs to handle peak hours, not just the average. If the peak is 5 cases between 8-12 and the robot can only do 3, you need two systems. This is how you size the fleet with data.
6
Robot Compatibility Matrix — "Which da Vinci model fits which surgery?"
The money slide. Scores every procedure type against every da Vinci model (dV5, Xi, X, SP) on a 0-100 fit scale. A radical prostatectomy scores 98 on the dV5 but only 75 on the X. A transoral procedure scores 95 on the SP but only 55 on the Xi. This tells the rep exactly which model to pitch based on what THIS hospital actually does. No guessing.
7
Design Day Analysis — "How big should we size the system?"
Borrowed from warehouse engineering. P50 = typical day. P75 = 75% of operating days at or below this. P90 = busy days. P95 = peak. The recommendation: size for P75 — handles most days comfortably without overpaying for capacity needed only 5% of the time. This is how you justify 1 system vs. 2 vs. 3 to the CFO with math, not feelings.
8
5-Year Volume Projection — "How fast will robotic adoption grow?"
Shows the ramp from year 1 (40% of convertible cases go robotic) to year 5 (85%+), broken down by specialty. Answers the CFO's question: "If we buy this, will usage grow or flatline?" Also shows when the hospital needs a SECOND system — which is a future sale for your rep.
9
Financial Deep Dive — "Show me the money"
What the CFO actually cares about. Total cost of ownership over 5 years (system + instruments + service + training + renovation). Per-procedure economics (robotic costs $2,200 more per case but generates $9,500 more in margin). The breakeven chart shows the exact month where cumulative benefits exceed costs. If breakeven is month 14, the CFO says: "We're profitable by Q2 of year 2." This replaces the hand-wavy Excel models every rep builds differently.
10
Growth Extrapolation — "What if growth is faster or slower?"
Three scenarios: conservative (10% annual growth), baseline (15%), aggressive (20%). Shows the range so the CFO isn't betting on one number. Also shows when fleet expansion is needed under each scenario — "under baseline, second system by year 3; under aggressive, by year 2." This de-risks the purchase decision because even the conservative scenario shows ROI.
11
System Recommendation — "Here's exactly what to buy"
The final answer: which model (dV5, Xi, X, or SP), how many systems, the fit score (0-100), and a plain-English rationale for WHY. Plus risk factors and mitigations. This is what the rep hands to the VP of Surgical Services and says: "This isn't my opinion. This is what the data says."

The Sales Problem

Why Hospital CFOs Keep Saying No — and What Fixes It

Intuitive trades at 54x earnings on the promise that robotic surgery produces better outcomes. But when a hospital CFO asks "prove it at our institution," the sales team has no answer. This is the core commercial friction — and the reason SurgicalMind AI is not a nice-to-have but existential infrastructure.

$
The ROI Proof Problem
CMS and commercial payers reimburse the same amount whether a hysterectomy is done laparoscopically or robotically. The hospital absorbs the $1,500-3,500/case cost premium (instruments, drapes, service). When you fully allocate depreciation + instruments + service contracts, a robotic case costs $3,000-6,000 MORE than the equivalent laparoscopic case. For 300 cases/year, that's $1-1.8M in incremental annual cost with no additional reimbursement.
What SurgicalMind AI Solves

Per-case outcomes tracking (ROM recovery speed, complications, readmissions, patient satisfaction) that proves robotic cases produce measurably better results at each specific institution. Not meta-analysis. Not industry averages. Their surgeons, their patients, their data. The CFO gets the ROI proof that Intuitive's sales team has never been able to provide.

?
The Clinical Evidence Gap
Prostatectomy: robotic advantage is settled. But for hysterectomy (JAMA 2019 — no significant difference vs. laparoscopic), colorectal (ROLARR trial, Lancet 2017 — no significant difference), and general surgery, multiple Cochrane reviews conclude "insufficient evidence of robotic superiority." Expert laparoscopists see no clinical need for a $2M instrument holder.
What SurgicalMind AI Solves

Real-time, institutional-level outcomes comparison: robotic vs. laparoscopic vs. open, same hospital, same procedure, same patient population. If the robot IS better — the data proves it and justifies expansion. If it isn't for certain procedures — the data redirects investment to where robotic surgery genuinely wins. Either way, hospitals make decisions based on evidence instead of marketing.

The Utilization Crisis
Average da Vinci utilization across the installed base is estimated at 2-4 cases/week. Breakeven is 250-350+ cases/year. Many community hospitals never reach it. If a hospital has only 1-2 credentialed surgeons and one leaves, utilization craters. Industry publications have documented hospitals that essentially mothball systems after 2-3 years — the "robot in the closet" phenomenon.
What SurgicalMind AI Solves

AI-powered patient triage identifies more surgical candidates from the existing patient population — patients who would benefit from robotic intervention but are currently being routed to laparoscopic or conservative treatment. Pre-op ROM assessment and risk stratification expand the clinical funnel. More qualified patients = higher utilization = better per-case economics.

Automated quality measure reporting, outcome benchmarking, and recovery timeline comparison (robotic vs. traditional) formatted for payer submission. Combined with $150-200K/yr service contracts, the ongoing cost frequently exceeds what administrators modeled at purchase time. The dV5 transition adds a second incompatible instrument inventory for mixed-fleet hospitals.
What SurgicalMind AI Solves

When per-case instrument costs are offset by provably better outcomes (shorter recovery, fewer readmissions, faster return to work), the total cost-of-care equation changes. SurgicalMind AI provides the outcome evidence that reframes the conversation from "instruments cost too much" to "the total cost of a better outcome is justified." This is the narrative shift Intuitive's sales team needs.
