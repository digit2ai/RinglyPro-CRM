Slide 4 on proposal — ABC Classification

The Pareto Principle — This Is the Money Slide

"This is ABC analysis — the 80/20 rule applied to the warehouse." "Just 1,051 products — that's half a percent of the catalog — drive 80% of all volume. Those are your A-items, the fast movers. That's where you put your goods-to-person automation." "184,000 products are dead stock — zero movement. The Gini coefficient of 0.897 confirms this is highly concentrated. That's ideal for automation." "Look at the table — it shows the exact percentages for order lines, pick units, and orders per class. Every number comes from the data. Nothing is hardcoded."
This is the slide Sven will relate to most — it matches what LogiVision does, but with more detail and zero manual effort.

Slide 6 on proposal — Order Profile

How Complex Are the Orders?

"88% of orders are multi-line — meaning each order picks multiple products. Average is about 10 lines per order." "That tells us this operation needs zone-based picking or wave automation, not simple single-item stations. The histogram shows the distribution."

Slide 7 on proposal — Percentiles (Design Basis)

The Design Basis — Most Important for Engineering

"This is the slide your engineers care about most." "Average day: 1,723 order lines. But we don't design for average. We design for the 75th percentile — 1,811 lines per day. That's about 151 lines per hour." "The max day hit 1,904. Designing to the 75th percentile ensures the system handles peak demand 75% of the time while remaining cost-effective."
If Sven asks "why 75th and not 90th?" — say "90th over-engineers the system and increases cost. 75th is the industry standard for warehouse automation dimensioning."
