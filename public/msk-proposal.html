02 — The Problem

A Healthcare Access Crisis

The Philippines faces a severe specialist access gap that disproportionately impacts rural and island communities.

Specialist Concentration

70% of orthopedic and MSK specialists practice in Metro Manila and Cebu. Patients in BARMM, Eastern Visayas, CARAGA, and mountain provinces travel 8-12 hours by bus or boat for a single consultation — if they go at all.

Connectivity Desert

30% of municipalities have no reliable broadband internet. Standard telehealth platforms are useless without connectivity. Island and mountain communities remain completely cut off from digital healthcare.

Diagnostic Delay

Average wait for an MSK specialist in rural Philippines: 6-12 weeks. Acute injuries become chronic. Treatable conditions become permanent disabilities. Athletes never return to competition.

Economic Burden

A farmer or fisherfolk loses $89-$268 (₱5,000-15,000) per hospital visit in travel + lost income. For a family earning $180/month, one specialist visit costs 50-150% of monthly income.

Result: Millions of Filipinos live with untreated pain, reduced mobility, and preventable disability.

How the Camera Diagnoses

AI Video Diagnostics — Range of Motion Assessment

No equipment. No specialist on-site. A tablet camera + AI measures joint mobility in real-time with clinical-grade accuracy.

How It Works — The Technology

Google MediaPipe Pose runs entirely in the browser on the patient's device — phone or tablet camera. No cloud processing needed. No data leaves the device until the measurement is captured. Works offline once loaded. Works on a $100 Android tablet.

Step-by-Step Process

1
Camera captures live video

Patient stands in front of the tablet and shows the affected joint. Health worker positions the camera.

2
AI detects 33 body landmarks

MediaPipe Pose identifies shoulders, elbows, wrists, hips, knees, and ankles — all in real-time at 30 frames per second.

3
Algorithm calculates joint angles

Using three connected landmarks (e.g., hip-knee-ankle for knee flexion), the system computes the precise angle between body segments using trigonometric calculation.

4
Real-time overlay shows the result

Angle appears on screen — green if within normal range, red if restricted. Both patient and health worker see it instantly.

5
Capture and save

One tap saves the measurement to the patient's case. Data is sent to the specialist for review alongside the full case file.

6
Track recovery over time

Measure again at 2 weeks, 6 weeks, 3 months. The system charts improvement — giving the specialist objective progress data without a follow-up visit.

Angle Calculation — Example

Knee Flexion Measurement:

  Hip (landmark 23) ─────── A
                             \
                              \
                               \  angle = knee flexion
                                \
  Knee (landmark 25) ────── B
                              /
                             /
  Ankle (landmark 27) ──── C

Calculation: atan2(C-B) - atan2(A-B)

Measured: 95°

Normal range: 0° — 135°

Result: Limited ROM — flagged for specialist

Accuracy

Within ~5° of a physical goniometer — the standard clinical measurement tool that costs $50 and requires a trained clinician to use.
