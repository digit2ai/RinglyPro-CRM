What is going on — in plain English

The overall picture is mostly reassuring. The transplant is healthy and the dangerous anemia from April has improved a lot. There are a few things that need steady attention — mainly the acid level in the blood and the kidney working at about half strength — but all of them have a clear plan.

The good news first. The transplanted kidney is not being rejected, two dangerous transplant viruses are both negative, the anti-rejection drug level is finally in the right range, the severe anemia is much better, and the CT scan of the head was completely normal.

Good news

Transplant is safe Good

CMV virus: Not detected. BK virus: Not detected. These are the two infections that most threaten a transplanted kidney — both negative.

Anti-rejection level fixed In range

Tacrolimus (Prograf) went from too low (3.4 in April) up to 7.2 — now in the target zone after switching to twice-a-day dosing.

Anemia much better Improving

Hemoglobin rose from a dangerous 7.1 in April to 11.3 now. The blood smear shows young new red cells — a sign the bone marrow is recovering.

Head CT normal Normal

The headache was scanned. The brain is normal — no bleeding, no mass, nothing dangerous.

No blood infection Clear

Blood culture grew nothing and the lactic acid was normal — no sign of a bloodstream infection or sepsis.

Nutrition holding up Normal

Albumin (a protein that shows nutrition) is normal at 3.98. Keep the protein going.

Needs attention

Blood is too acidic Low CO₂ 14.3

The bicarbonate (CO₂) is low, meaning the blood is too acidic. This is common when a transplant kidney is weaker. It causes tiredness and can wear on the kidney and muscles. The fix is already prescribed: sodium bicarbonate and Bicitra — take them every day.

Kidney at about half strength eGFR 53

The transplant kidney is filtering at about 53% (Stage 3). Mild dehydration signs too (high BUN ratio). Stay well hydrated and keep transplant follow-ups.

Protein & blood in urine Protein 2+

The kidney is leaking a little protein and blood. Not an emergency, but the kidney/transplant doctor should track it.

Blood sugar a bit high Glucose 153

Often caused by the steroid (prednisone). Farxiga helps protect against this. Worth a recheck and a mention to the doctor.

Immune system turned down Low WBC 3.82

White cells and lymphocytes are low — this is expected from the anti-rejection medicines. It means infections must be taken seriously: call the doctor for any fever.

Stay hydrated BUN/Creat 25

The blood chemistry hints at not enough fluids. More water helps the kidney and the acid level — confirm your daily fluid target with the transplant team.

Next steps — how to fix this

  • Take every medicine on schedule — no skips.Especially the 4 anti-rejection meds and the 2 acid-fixing meds (sodium bicarbonate 3×/day + Bicitra). This is the single most important thing.
  • Drink more fluids.The blood chemistry shows mild dehydration. More water helps the kidney and the acid level. Ask the transplant team for a daily fluid target (some transplant patients have limits).
  • Recheck the chemistry panel (CMP) in a few weeks.Specifically to confirm the bicarbonate (CO₂ 14.3) is climbing back toward normal once the bicarbonate meds are taken consistently.
  • Call the transplant / kidney team about three things.The low bicarbonate (14.3), the kidney function (eGFR 53), and the protein & blood in the urine. Dr. Anwar (transplant) and Dr. Pimentel (primary) should know.
  • Recheck the blood count (CBC).To confirm the hemoglobin keeps rising past 11.3 and the low white count stays stable.
  • Watch the blood sugar.Glucose was 153. Mention it to the doctor — it may just be the steroid, but it should be rechecked.
  • Protect against infection.Because the immune system is turned down, avoid sick contacts, wash hands often, and call the doctor right away for fever, chills, burning urine, or feeling suddenly worse.
  • Keep eating protein at every meal.Albumin is normal now (3.98) — protein keeps the blood, muscles, and healing strong. Don't let it slip.
