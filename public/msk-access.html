The Problem

44 million Filipinos lack access to specialist care

In remote provinces, a knee injury means weeks of travel, lost income, and often no diagnosis at all.

Specialist Concentration

70% of orthopedic specialists are in Metro Manila and Cebu. Patients in BARMM, Eastern Visayas, and CARAGA travel 8-12 hours for a consultation.

Connectivity Desert

30% of Philippine municipalities have no reliable broadband. Island and mountain communities can't access telehealth even when platforms exist.

⏳

Diagnostic Delay

Average wait for an MSK specialist in rural Philippines: 6-12 weeks. Many injuries worsen, become chronic, or lead to permanent disability.

💰

Economic Burden

A farmer or fisherfolk loses ₱5,000-15,000 in travel + lost income per hospital visit. Many choose to live with pain rather than seek care.

Real Impact

How it works for a real patient

A farmer in Samar with a bad knee — today vs. with MSK Access.

👨‍🌾

Mang Pedro, 52 — Rice Farmer, Samar

Right knee pain for 3 months. Can't kneel to plant. Nearest orthopedic specialist: Tacloban (4 hours by bus).

1
Walks to the barangay health center

5 minutes from his farm. The health worker opens MSK Intelligence on the tablet.

2
Talks to Dr. MSK

"Masakit ang tuhod ko pag naglalakad" — the AI understands, asks follow-up questions, records everything.

3
Shows his knee on camera

AI measures: 95° flexion (normal: 135°). Flags limited range. Captures measurement automatically.

4
AI triages the case

Chronic overuse, moderate severity. Recommends MRI protocol. Routes to specialist queue.

5
Specialist reviews within 24 hours

Board-certified radiologist in Manila reviews the case. Diagnoses early osteoarthritis. Prescribes exercise program.

6
Pedro follows his exercise program

Opens the app on his phone. 5 exercises, 3x/week. Pain score drops from 7 to 3 in 6 weeks. Back to planting.
