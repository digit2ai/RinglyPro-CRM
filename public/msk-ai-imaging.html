Findings

Sagittal X-ray view of the right index finger demonstrates a complete transverse fracture through the proximal phalanx with significant dorsal angulation and displacement. The fracture appears to be located in the mid to distal third of the proximal phalanx. There is notable shortening and override of the fracture fragments. Impression

ICD-10 Codes

W20.8XXA Other cause of strike by thrown, projected or falling object, initial encounter

Recommended Follow-Up

Urgent orthopedic consultation for fracture reduction and fixation. Post-reduction imaging to confirm alignment. Consider CT if surgical planning needed for complex reconstruction.

Limitations: Single view provided limits full assessment of fracture pattern and alignment in all planes. Additional orthogonal views would be beneficial for complete evaluation and surgical planning.

Clinical Summary

48-year-old patient sustained a comminuted displaced fracture of the right index finger proximal phalanx from crush injury during tree cutting accident. Multiple imaging views confirm significant fragment displacement, dorsal angulation, and soft tissue trauma requiring orthopedic intervention.

Detailed Findings

Multiple radiographic views demonstrate a comminuted fracture of the proximal phalanx of the right index finger with significant displacement and dorsal angulation. The fracture pattern shows multiple bone fragments with loss of normal cortical architecture, consistent with a crush injury mechanism. There is notable shortening and override of fracture fragments with disruption of the metacarpophalangeal joint alignment. Extensive soft tissue swelling surrounds the injury site. The middle and distal phalanges remain intact, and the remaining metacarpals and carpal bones show no evidence of fracture. Joint spaces in uninjured digits are preserved. The fracture extends through the mid to distal third of the proximal phalanx with complete cortical disruption.

Diagnostic Impression

Comminuted displaced fracture of the right index finger proximal phalanx secondary to crush injury. The fracture pattern with significant angulation and displacement indicates need for orthopedic surgical evaluation for possible open reduction and internal fixation.

Recovery Plan

Expected recovery involves surgical fixation followed by 6-8 weeks of immobilization, then progressive hand therapy for 4-6 weeks. Full functional recovery may take 3-4 months with potential for permanent grip strength reduction and finger stiffness.

05 -- Technical Architecture

Designed for Reliability and Speed

Non-Blocking Upload

The upload returns immediately. AI analysis runs in the background. By the time you scroll down, the analysis is usually already complete.

Persistent Database Storage

Images are stored in the PostgreSQL database, not on temporary filesystems. Images survive server restarts and infrastructure changes.

Clinical Context Injection

Before analysis, the system gathers the patient's clinical data. A knee image from a trauma case is interpreted differently than one from a degenerative case.

Structured JSON Output

The AI returns a defined schema that feeds into the copilot, Lina voice assistant, PDF exports, and the case timeline -- not unstructured free text.
