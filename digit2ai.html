01 — The Opportunity

A once-in-a-generation window for SMB AI.

33 million U.S. small businesses still run on phone calls, spreadsheets, and manual work. Four converging forces have opened the door for a new class of AI-native operators.

01 / Cost collapse

AI inference down 90%+

LLM costs fell over 90% in 18 months. Voice AI that cost $1/minute in 2023 now costs pennies — making full-stack AI viable for $500/mo SMB subscriptions.

02 / Adoption inflection

SMB AI adoption crossed 15%

From under 5% to 15%+ in 2025. Early adopters see ROI, word-of-mouth pulls industries forward. We're at the bottom of the S-curve.

03 / Behavior shift

Voice AI went mainstream

Consumers now expect AI on the phone. Businesses without 24/7 AI response lose leads nightly. SMBs need tools to keep up — fast.

04 / First-mover

Verticals with zero competition

No one builds AI-native platforms for dairy plants, martial arts schools, or lawn care. Big tech chases enterprise. We own these niches first.

05 / Bilingual moat

Bilingual from day one

Full English + Spanish across voice, UI, and operations. Unlocks 62M+ Hispanic Americans and all of LATAM — a market most competitors treat as an afterthought.

06 / Shared infrastructure

New verticals in weeks

Each new platform starts at 60% complete thanks to shared infra: voice, auth, billing, CRM, MCP, dashboards. Velocity compounds.

04 — Business Model

Three compounding revenue streams.

Every platform we ship feeds the same financial machine — recurring subscriptions, usage-based expansion, and vertical licensing. New verticals deepen the moat.

See financial detail
1

SaaS subscriptions

Monthly recurring revenue across all 15 deployed platforms. Voice agents, CRM access, dashboards, automation — billed per tenant.

2

Usage-based revenue

Per-call and per-message fees on AI voice agents. As clients grow, usage grows — built-in expansion with zero upselling.

3

Vertical licensing

Each industry platform can be white-labeled and licensed across regions, operators, and franchise networks.

Zero-copy
customer data.

One CRM core, 15 vertical front-doors. Customer records, voice transcripts, appointments, and ops data stay in a single multi-tenant graph — never replicated, never fragmented. Every new vertical gets the full intelligence of the last one, instantly.

05 — Architecture

One brain. Many front-doors.

Our MCP-powered infrastructure means every platform inherits voice, memory, billing, and automation — without duplicating a single customer record. This is how we add verticals in weeks, not years.
