**What Snowflake offers Intuitive: a medical chatbot on top of the warehouse.**

- Snowflake stores Intuitive's hospital, surgeon, and rep data
- The AI piece — Cortex / their conversational layer — sits on top of that warehouse
- Answers natural-language questions: "list hospitals in Florida with more than 8,000 surgical cases and a robotic adoption under 20%"
- Useful, but functionally it's a domain-specific chatbot
- A rep types a question, the chatbot returns a row set or a sentence
- Nothing past the answer is built

**What ThoughtSpot offers Intuitive: an analytics layer.**

- Reads from the same Snowflake (or any warehouse)
- Renders the answer as charts, dashboards, and "liveboards"
- The modern version of Tableau or Power BI, driven by natural-language search
- Reps see ranked lists, trend lines, KPI tiles
- Same data, prettier surface
- Still no workflow — visualizing the answer is not the same as generating the proposal, sending the surgeon survey, or tracking the deal

So the pilot Intuitive is committing to is: a medical chatbot, plus an analytics dashboard. Two tools. Each priced per user. Neither one drafts a CFO presentation, sends a magic-link survey to a surgeon, builds a proforma, or tracks plan-vs-actuals after the system is placed. Reps still do all of that in PowerPoint, Excel, Outlook, and a notebook.

**What SurgicalMind offers Intuitive: the all-in-one sales app.**

- One product. One login. One screen for the rep.
- Built end-to-end for a commercial rep working a hospital deal from cold contact through signed contract through plan tracking after placement
- Snowflake's chatbot and ThoughtSpot's analytics are not next to SurgicalMind — they are inside it
- Already deployed at thirteen pilot hospitals

**Step 1 — Hospital Intake (replaces the Snowflake pilot scope entirely):**
