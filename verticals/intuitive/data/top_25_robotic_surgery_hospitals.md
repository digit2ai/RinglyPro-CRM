---

## Methodology & Limitations

Individual hospital robotic surgery volumes are **not publicly reported** in a standardized way. There is no CMS dataset, no Intuitive Surgical public filing, and no single ranking source that lists all hospitals by robotic case volume. CMS does not separately track robotic vs. laparoscopic approaches in claims data (they share CPT codes in many cases).

This report is assembled from: hospital press releases, milestone announcements, Becker's Hospital Review, Intuitive Surgical investor filings (10-K, quarterly earnings), academic publications, AHA data, and news articles. Cumulative vs. annual figures are distinguished where possible. **All estimated values are flagged with [EST].**

## Specialty Robotic-to-Open Ratios (U.S. National Averages)

### Urology
- **Radical prostatectomy**: 94.8% robotic, ~3% open, ~2% laparoscopic (2021 data; up from 83% in 2014)
- Only 23% of urologists still perform any open prostatectomy (down from 46% in 2014)
- Overall urology: ~40-45% of all procedures robotic [EST]
- 86% of U.S. urology residency programs have a da Vinci system
- **Source**: AUA (AUANews, Feb 2024)

### Gynecology
- **Hysterectomy**: ~61% performed robotically (2018); ~65% minimally invasive overall
- ~43% of MIS hysterectomies are robotic-assisted
- For morbidly obese patients, robotic hysterectomy is #1 approach at 40.8% (2020)
- All 42 U.S. GYN oncology fellowship programs have da Vinci access
- Overall GYN: ~25-30% of all procedures robotic [EST]
- **Source**: PMC/Journal of Robotic Surgery, AAGL

### General Surgery
- ~20-30% of general surgical procedures now robotic [EST]
- Robotic colectomy: grew from 1.5% (2012) to ~15-20% (2024) [EST]
- Cholecystectomy and hernia repair: fastest growing robotic categories
- **Source**: Intuitive earnings calls, iData Research, PMC

## Data Quality Summary

| Data Point | Availability | Notes |
|------------|-------------|-------|
| Total staffed beds | HIGH (70%) | Available from AHA, Definitive Healthcare, hospital websites |
| Number of surgeons | LOW (20%) | Rarely disclosed; usually total physicians reported instead |
| da Vinci system count | MODERATE (50%) | From press releases; Intuitive does not publish per-site data |
| da Vinci model | MODERATE (45%) | Known when hospitals announce new acquisitions |
| Annual robotic volume | LOW-MODERATE (40%) | Some from press releases; many not disclosed |
| Annual open volume | VERY LOW (<5%) | Almost never separately reported alongside robotic |
| Robotic:Open ratio by specialty | NATIONAL ONLY | No hospital-level breakdown found; only national averages available |
