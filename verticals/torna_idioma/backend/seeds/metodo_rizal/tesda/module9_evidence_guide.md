# Evidence Guide — Module 9: Media and Technology

## Unit of Competency
Communicate about media, technology and information in Spanish (CEFR B2)

This unit covers the knowledge, skills and attitudes required to understand, discuss and produce
spoken and written Spanish about news media, social media, technology, the influence of media,
advertising/persuasion, and connectivity. At B2 the learner sustains accurate present/past
contrast, uses the passive voice (periphrastic and impersonal/pasiva refleja), forms the
subjunctive in recommendations, and selects prepositions of location and connectivity correctly.
Working language of this guide is English; learner-facing target performance is in Spanish.
Tagalog learner-facing wording is review-gated (G3).

---

## Elements & Performance Criteria

### Element 9.1 — Media and the news (present/past contrast)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Names and describes core news media (la prensa, el titular, el noticiero, la emisora) in Spanish.
  2. Contrasts how media were consumed in the past with how they are consumed now, using
     imperfect (antes leía…) against present (ahora recibo…).
  3. Reports the content of a headline or broadcast accurately in own words.
  4. Sustains the present/past contrast across at least three connected sentences without
     breaking tense agreement.
  5. Uses media word-family derivations correctly (periodista → periódico → periodismo).
- **Linked roots:** m9-noticia, m9-periodista, m9-titular, m9-emisora, m9-prensa, m9-transmitir,
  m9-buscador, m9-grabar, m9-aplicación

### Element 9.2 — Social media (passive voice; DO/IO with communication verbs)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Describes social-media actions (publicar, compartir, comentar, seguir) in Spanish.
  2. Reformulates an active statement into the periphrastic passive (La foto fue publicada por…).
  3. Uses direct- and indirect-object pronouns with communication verbs correctly
     (le dejé un comentario y él me lo respondió).
  4. Identifies the agent (por + agente) when relevant and omits it when not.
  5. Talks about a profile, followers and engagement coherently across a short exchange.
- **Linked roots:** m9-publicar, m9-seguidor, m9-compartir, m9-comentario, m9-perfil

### Element 9.3 — Technology vocabulary (descriptive adjectives/adverbs)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Names common devices and their parts (dispositivo, pantalla, batería, programa) in Spanish.
  2. Describes a device with precise adjectives and intensifying adverbs
     (increíblemente rápido, sorprendentemente ligero).
  3. Places adjectives correctly relative to the noun and makes them agree in gender/number.
  4. Compares two devices/programs using descriptive language.
  5. Explains how something behaves over time, contrasting older and newer technology
     (antes actualizaba… ahora actualizo…).
- **Linked roots:** m9-dispositivo, m9-pantalla, m9-programa, m9-inteligente, m9-batería,
  m9-actualizar

### Element 9.4 — The influence of the media (impersonal passive)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Discusses the influence of media on opinion using the impersonal/pasiva refleja
     (se dice que…, se difunden…, se manipula…).
  2. Explains how rumors and hoaxes (bulos) spread without naming an explicit agent.
  3. Distinguishes reliable information from manipulated or false information in Spanish.
  4. States generalized claims about audiences and reach impersonally.
  5. Sustains se + verb agreement (singular vs. plural) with the affected noun.
- **Linked roots:** m9-influir, m9-difundir, m9-manipular, m9-bulo, m9-audiencia

### Element 9.5 — Advertising and persuasion (subjunctive in recommendations)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Gives advice and recommendations about advertising and consumption using the present
     subjunctive (te recomiendo que evites…, sugiero que compare…).
  2. Triggers the subjunctive after recommendation/advice verbs and after es importante que…
  3. Warns a consumer about misleading ads or manipulative persuasion in Spanish.
  4. Justifies a recommendation with para que + subjunctive (para que el cliente confíe…).
  5. Maintains person/number agreement in the subordinate subjunctive clause.
- **Linked roots:** m9-anuncio, m9-publicidad, m9-marca, m9-persuadir, m9-consumidor

### Element 9.6 — Tech expressions (prepositions of location/connectivity)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Describes connecting, downloading and storing using correct prepositions
     (conectarse a internet, descargar desde la tienda, guardar en la nube).
  2. States where one is online or located using en/desde/a (estoy en la red social).
  3. Communicates by email/chat naming the channel and destination correctly
     (escribir por correo, dejar en la carpeta compartida).
  4. Gives basic security guidance about passwords using prepositional phrases of place.
  5. Selects the correct preposition for source (desde), destination (a), and location (en).
- **Linked roots:** m9-conectar, m9-red, m9-descargar, m9-correo, m9-contraseña

## Oral / Written Question Bank

### Element 9.1 (key grammar: present/past contrast — imperfect vs. present)
1. **[9.1]** Q: When do you use the imperfect rather than the preterite for the past in this topic?
   A: For habitual or ongoing past actions and states (how news *used to* be consumed), e.g.
   *antes leía las noticias en el periódico*; the present marks the current habit
   (*ahora las recibo en el móvil*).
2. **[9.1]** Q: Give the word family of *periodista*.
   A: periodista → periódico → periodismo → periodístico.
3. **[9.1]** Q: How would you contrast past and present broadcasting of a match?
   A: *Antes los partidos se transmitían por televisión, pero ahora los transmiten en directo
   por internet.*
4. **[9.1]** Q: What is a *titular* and how does it differ from a *noticia*?
   A: A *titular* is the headline (the short title); a *noticia* is the full news item/story.

### Element 9.2 (key grammar: passive voice; DO/IO with communication verbs)
1. **[9.2]** Q: How do you form the periphrastic passive in Spanish?
   A: ser + past participle (agreeing in gender/number) + optional *por* + agent, e.g.
   *La foto fue publicada por la influencer.*
2. **[9.2]** Q: Rewrite "Miles de cuentas compartieron el vídeo" in the passive.
   A: *El vídeo fue compartido por miles de cuentas.*
3. **[9.2]** Q: In *se lo respondió*, what do *se* and *lo* stand for?
   A: *se* = indirect object (to me/him → becomes *se* before *lo*), *lo* = direct object
   (the comment/answer). DO/IO combine as *se lo*.
4. **[9.2]** Q: When can you omit the agent in a passive sentence?
   A: When the agent is unknown, obvious or unimportant, e.g. *Su perfil fue verificado*
   (by the platform — left out if not needed).

### Element 9.3 (key grammar: descriptive adjectives/adverbs)
1. **[9.3]** Q: How are most manner adverbs formed in Spanish?
   A: feminine adjective + *-mente*, e.g. rápida → rápidamente, increíble → increíblemente.
2. **[9.3]** Q: Describe a screen using two adjectives.
   A: *La pantalla es muy nítida y los colores se ven vivos.*
3. **[9.3]** Q: Where do descriptive adjectives normally go relative to the noun?
   A: Usually after the noun (*un programa intuitivo*), agreeing in gender/number.
4. **[9.3]** Q: Contrast how often you update a system now vs. before.
   A: *Antes actualizaba el sistema una vez al año, pero ahora lo actualizo cada semana.*

### Element 9.4 (key grammar: impersonal passive / pasiva refleja)
1. **[9.4]** Q: How do you form the impersonal passive with *se*?
   A: *se* + verb agreeing with the affected noun, e.g. *se difunde un rumor* (sg) /
   *se difunden rumores* (pl).
2. **[9.4]** Q: Make "people say the media influence the young" impersonal.
   A: *Se dice que los medios influyen mucho en la opinión de los jóvenes.*
3. **[9.4]** Q: Why use the impersonal passive when talking about hoaxes?
   A: To state what happens generally without naming who does it, e.g.
   *Se comparten muchos bulos durante las campañas.*
4. **[9.4]** Q: Singular or plural — "Se ___ mucha información falsa"?
   A: *Se manipula* (singular, agrees with *información*).

### Element 9.5 (key grammar: subjunctive in recommendations)
1. **[9.5]** Q: Which verbs trigger the subjunctive in a recommendation clause?
   A: recomendar que, aconsejar que, sugerir que, and expressions like *es importante que* —
   the subordinate verb goes to the present subjunctive (*que evites*, *que compare*).
2. **[9.5]** Q: Advise someone to avoid misleading ads.
   A: *Te recomiendo que evites los anuncios engañosos que prometen riqueza fácil.*
3. **[9.5]** Q: Complete: "Es importante que la publicidad ___ honesta."
   A: *sea* (present subjunctive of *ser*).
4. **[9.5]** Q: How do you express purpose with the subjunctive here?
   A: *para que* + subjunctive, e.g. *…para que el cliente confíe en la marca.*

### Element 9.6 (key grammar: prepositions of location/connectivity)
1. **[9.6]** Q: Which preposition for the source of a download, and which for destination?
   A: source = *desde* (*descargo la aplicación desde la tienda*); destination/place =
   *en* (*la instalo en el teléfono*).
2. **[9.6]** Q: Say you connect to the internet and store files in the cloud.
   A: *Me conecto a internet desde casa y guardo mis archivos en la nube.*
3. **[9.6]** Q: Which preposition: "Estoy ___ la red social"?
   A: *en* (location within the network).
4. **[9.6]** Q: Give one password tip using a prepositional phrase of place.
   A: *Guarda la contraseña en un lugar seguro y no la pongas en notas visibles.*

## Critical Aspects of Competency
The learner must demonstrate ALL of the following for overall competence:
- Sustains accurate **present/past contrast** (imperfect vs. present) when describing media/tech
  change (Elements 9.1, 9.3).
- Forms the **periphrastic passive** correctly (ser + participle, agreement, optional agent) and
  uses **DO/IO pronouns** with communication verbs (Element 9.2).
- Produces the **impersonal passive / pasiva refleja** with correct *se* + verb agreement
  (Element 9.4).
- Triggers and forms the **present subjunctive in recommendations/advice**, including *para que*
  and *es importante que* (Element 9.5).
- Selects the correct **prepositions of location and connectivity** (a / desde / en / por) in
  technology contexts (Element 9.6).
- Uses Module 9 vocabulary (the Cinco Raíces roots) accurately and with appropriate word-family
  derivations across spoken and written tasks.
- Communicates at **CEFR B2** — coherent, connected discourse with self-correction, not isolated
  memorized phrases.

## Methods of Assessment
Competence is assessed through a combination of:
- **Demonstration / observation** — learner performs the can-do tasks in the checklist above
  (oral description, role-play, paraphrase) while the rater marks S / NY.
- **Oral questioning** — underpinning-knowledge questions from the bank, tagged by element,
  confirming the learner *knows why* (grammar rationale), not only *how*.
- **Portfolio of Cinco Raíces "can-do" summaries** — the learner's per-session self-recorded
  "can-do" entries for the 35 Module 9 roots, evidencing spaced retrieval (SM-2 SRS) and
  productive use in example sentences.
- **Written task** — a short B2 text (e.g. an opinion paragraph on media influence using the
  impersonal passive, or a set of consumer recommendations using the subjunctive) drawn from
  Elements 9.4 and 9.5.

All four methods together cover knowledge, skills and attitudes; no single method is sufficient
on its own. No legal-sufficiency claim is made; this guide supports instructional-designer review.

## Context of Assessment
- **Where:** classroom or supervised online session (proctored), individually or in pairs for
  role-play tasks.
- **Conditions:** quiet environment; audio for oral tasks; the learner may use the Cinco Raíces
  vocabulary reference for the written task but not for live oral demonstration.
- **Tools allowed:** device/screen for prompts and the written task; no live machine translation
  during target performance.
- **Language of instruction:** English (en) for rubric and questioning; Tagalog (fil) support
  permitted for clarification `[FIL — pending G3 review]`.
- **Language of target performance:** **Spanish (es) is required** for all demonstration and
  written outputs that evidence the unit.
