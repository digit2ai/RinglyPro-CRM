# Evidence Guide — Module 5: Your Habits Speak for You

## Unit of Competency

**Discuss leisure, preferences and social plans in Spanish.** (CEFR A2–B1)

The candidate can talk about free time and hobbies, react to films and music, describe
sporting ability, make and respond to invitations with appropriate courtesy, express
opinions and preferences, and arrange social plans by date and time — all in spoken and
written Spanish at an A2→B1 level. Tagalog (Filipino) may be used by the rater for
clarification only; the target performance is in Spanish.

**Key grammar exercised in this unit (from the competency map):**
- Reflexive verbs across tenses (present, present perfect, preterite) — *me divierto / me he
  acostumbrado / me relajé*
- Preterite vs. present perfect contrast — *ayer vi / esta semana he visto*
- Modal verbs of ability and possibility — *poder, saber* (+ infinitive)
- Conditional for wishes and courtesy — *me gustaría, ¿podría…?, ¿le importaría…?*
- Present subjunctive for opinions, preferences and reactions — *no creo que sea, prefiero
  que vayamos, dudo que guste, me alegro de que…*
- Prepositions of date and time — *el sábado por la noche, a las ocho, en mayo, el 19 de junio*

## Elements & Performance Criteria

### Element 5.1 — Free time and hobbies (reflexives across tenses)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Names at least four leisure activities / hobbies in Spanish using the correct article
     and gender.
  2. Describes what he/she does to relax or have fun using reflexive verbs in the present
     tense (*me divierto, me relajo, me dedico*).
  3. Reports a past or ongoing habit using a reflexive verb in the present perfect
     (*me he acostumbrado a…*).
  4. Sustains a 4–6 turn exchange about a hobby, giving frequency and a reason.
- **Linked roots:** `m5-divertirse`, `m5-relajarse`, `m5-aficion`, `m5-dedicarse`,
  `m5-pasatiempo`, `m5-acostumbrarse`, `m5-soler`, `m5-aburrirse`, `m5-disfrutar`

### Element 5.2 — Cinema and music (preterite vs. present perfect)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Talks about a film or song using the present perfect for life-experience / unfinished
     time (*he visto, he escuchado, nunca he cantado*).
  2. Narrates a specific past event using the preterite with a finished-time marker
     (*ayer vi, anoche canté, la semana pasada estrenó*).
  3. Contrasts both tenses correctly within one statement (*esta semana he visto…, pero
     ayer vi…*).
  4. Gives a short opinion of the film/song and a reason.
- **Linked roots:** `m5-pelicula`, `m5-cantar`, `m5-estrenar`, `m5-escuchar`, `m5-grupo`

### Element 5.3 — Sports and the outdoors (modal verbs: ability / possibility)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. States what he/she can and cannot do physically using *poder* + infinitive
     (*puedo nadar, no puedo correr…*).
  2. States an acquired skill using *saber* + infinitive and distinguishes it from *poder*
     (*sé montar en bicicleta*).
  3. Expresses obligation/necessity for training using *deber/tener que* + infinitive.
  4. Names at least three sports/outdoor activities and where they are practised.
- **Linked roots:** `m5-poder`, `m5-saber`, `m5-entrenar`, `m5-deporte`, `m5-montar`

### Element 5.4 — Events and shows (conditional for wishes and courtesy)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Expresses a wish to attend an event using the conditional (*me gustaría, nos
     encantaría*).
  2. Makes a polite request to buy/reserve using courtesy conditional forms
     (*¿podría…?, ¿le importaría…?*).
  3. Invites another person to an event courteously (*¿te gustaría acompañarme a…?*).
  4. Responds appropriately (accept / decline politely) to an invitation.
- **Linked roots:** `m5-gustar`, `m5-entrada`, `m5-espectaculo`, `m5-reservar`,
  `m5-acompanar`

### Element 5.5 — Opinions and preferences (subjunctive)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Gives an opinion using a verb of opinion that triggers the subjunctive in the negative
     (*no creo que sea…*).
  2. States a preference that another person act, using *preferir que* + subjunctive
     (*prefiero que vayamos…*).
  3. Expresses doubt or an emotional reaction with the subjunctive (*dudo que guste,
     me alegro de que…*).
  4. Compares two leisure options and justifies the preference.
- **Linked roots:** `m5-opinar`, `m5-preferir`, `m5-parecer`, `m5-dudar`, `m5-alegrarse`,
  `m5-interesar`

### Element 5.6 — Plans and invitations (prepositions of date and time)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Invites someone to an event giving the day, part of day and clock time using correct
     prepositions (*el sábado por la noche, a las ocho*).
  2. Proposes / confirms a meet-up time and place (*¿quedamos a las cinco… en la plaza?*).
  3. States a calendar date and month correctly (*el 19 de junio, en mayo*).
  4. Negotiates and agrees on a plan over a short dialogue (3+ turns).
- **Linked roots:** `m5-invitar`, `m5-quedar`, `m5-cita`, `m5-celebrar`, `m5-fecha`

## Oral / Written Question Bank

Underpinning-knowledge questions with model answers. Each is tagged with its element code.

### Element 5.1
1. **[5.1]** Why is *divertirse* called a reflexive verb, and how does it change with *yo*?
   — *Because the action returns to the subject; it needs a reflexive pronoun. With* yo
   *it becomes* me divierto.
2. **[5.1]** Conjugate *relajarse* for *nosotros* in the present. — *Nos relajamos.*
3. **[5.1]** Put *acostumbrarse* into the present perfect for *yo*. — *Me he acostumbrado*
   (*reflexive pronoun before* he*, past participle invariable*).
4. **[5.1]** What does *soler* + infinitive express? — *A habitual action: "usually do
   something," e.g.* suelo reunirme *= I usually meet up.*

### Element 5.2
1. **[5.2]** When do you use the present perfect rather than the preterite in Spain-style
   Spanish? — *For unfinished time periods or life experience (*hoy, esta semana, nunca,
   alguna vez*):* he visto, nunca he cantado.
2. **[5.2]** When is the preterite correct instead? — *For a completed action in a finished
   time (*ayer, anoche, la semana pasada*):* ayer vi, anoche canté.
3. **[5.2]** Correct this: *"Ayer he visto una película."* — *"Ayer **vi** una película"*
   (*finished-time marker* ayer *requires preterite*).
4. **[5.2]** Give the present perfect and preterite of *escuchar* (yo). — *He escuchado /
   escuché.*

### Element 5.3
1. **[5.3]** What is the difference between *poder* and *saber* before an infinitive? —
   *Saber* + inf. = know how to (a learned skill); poder + inf. = be able to / have the
   possibility right now.
2. **[5.3]** Why is *poder* called irregular? — *It is stem-changing* o→ue *(*puedo, puede,
   pueden*) but* podemos *keeps the* o.
3. **[5.3]** How do you say "I must train three times a week"? — *Debo entrenar tres veces
   por semana.*
4. **[5.3]** Translate "She knows how to ride a horse but cannot today." — *Sabe montar a
   caballo, pero hoy no puede.*

### Element 5.4
1. **[5.4]** Why use the conditional *me gustaría* instead of *quiero*? — *It is softer and
   more polite — a wish rather than a demand.*
2. **[5.4]** Make this request polite: *"Quiero dos entradas."* — *"¿**Podría** comprar dos
   entradas, por favor?"*
3. **[5.4]** How do you invite a friend to come with you, courteously? — *¿Te gustaría
   acompañarme a…?*
4. **[5.4]** Give a very polite way to ask someone to reserve a table. — *¿Le importaría
   reservar una mesa…?*

### Element 5.5
1. **[5.5]** Why does *no creo que* take the subjunctive? — *Negated opinion expresses doubt
   / non-assertion, which triggers the subjunctive:* no creo que sea.
2. **[5.5]** Give the subjunctive after *prefiero que* with *ir* (nosotros). — *Prefiero que
   **vayamos**.*
3. **[5.5]** Which of these needs the subjunctive: *"Creo que…"* or *"Dudo que…"*? — *"Dudo
   que…"* (*doubt → subjunctive; affirmative* creo que *takes indicative*).
4. **[5.5]** Complete: *"Me alegro de que tú ___ (preferir) salir."* — *prefieras.*

### Element 5.6
1. **[5.6]** Which preposition marks a clock time, and which marks a part of the day? —
   *a* (*a las ocho*) *for clock time;* por *(*por la noche, por la mañana*) *for part of
   the day.*
2. **[5.6]** How do you say a date like "June 19th"? — *El 19 de junio* (*el + number + de +
   month*).
3. **[5.6]** Which preposition is used with months alone? — *en* (*en mayo*).
4. **[5.6]** Propose meeting at 5 p.m. at the plaza on Sunday. — *¿Quedamos a las cinco de
   la tarde en la plaza el domingo?*

## Critical Aspects of Competency

The candidate must, to be judged competent overall:
- Use **reflexive verbs correctly across at least two tenses** (present and present perfect)
  when describing habits and leisure.
- **Distinguish preterite from present perfect** appropriately, driven by the time marker,
  in talking about cinema/music.
- Use **modal verbs *poder* and *saber*** accurately to express ability vs. learned skill.
- Make and respond to invitations with **courtesy conditional forms** (*me gustaría,
  ¿podría…?*).
- Form the **present subjunctive** correctly after opinion, preference, doubt and emotion
  triggers.
- Place **dates and times with the correct prepositions** when arranging a plan.
- Sustain interaction in Spanish across short multi-turn exchanges for each element, with
  errors that do not impede communication.

## Methods of Assessment

- **Demonstration / role-play** — observed performance of the can-do tasks in the checklist
  (hobby talk, film/music narration, sports ability, invitation role-play, opinion exchange,
  plan negotiation).
- **Oral questioning** — the underpinning-knowledge question bank, asked during or after the
  demonstration to confirm the candidate understands the grammar, not just memorised lines.
- **Portfolio of "Cinco Raíces" can-do summaries** — the candidate's session records for the
  35 Module 5 roots, including SRS progress and self-authored example sentences per element.
- **Written task** — a short composition (e.g. an invitation + plan, or an opinion paragraph
  on cinema vs. reading) that evidences the subjunctive, conditional and date/time
  prepositions in writing.
