# Evidence Guide — Module 1: Introduction to Spanish (Introducción al Español)

## Unit of Competency

**Communicate basic personal and social information in Spanish (CEFR A1)**

The learner greets, introduces self and others, gives basic personal and family information,
spells and counts, describes people physically, states origin and nationality, and uses core
courtesy expressions in Spanish — controlling the foundational grammar of the module (`ser`
vs `estar`, the regular present tense of `-ar`/`-ir` verbs, the irregular verb `tener`, and
the definite articles `el / la / los / las`).

**Key grammar (underpinning the unit):** `ser` vs `estar` · regular present tense · `tener`
· definite articles.

**Cinco Raíces coverage:** 40 vocabulary roots across 8 sessions (5 roots/session), mapped to
elements 1.1–1.6. Source: `module1_vocab_roots.json`.

---

## Elements & Performance Criteria

### Element 1.1 — Greetings, farewells and introductions

- **Performance criteria**
  1. Greets and takes leave appropriately for the time of day (e.g. *buenos días / buenas
     tardes / hasta luego*).
  2. Introduces self by name using `llamarse` (*me llamo…*) and asks another's name
     (*¿cómo te llamas?*).
  3. Introduces a third person using `presentar` (*te presento a…*).
  4. States identity and current state correctly, choosing `ser` for identity/role
     (*soy estudiante*) and `estar` for state (*estoy bien*).
- **Linked roots:** `m1-saludar`, `m1-llamarse`, `m1-ser`, `m1-estar`, `m1-presentar`,
  `m1-nombre`

### Element 1.2 — Names, days, the alphabet and numbers

- **Performance criteria**
  1. Spells own name and surname aloud in Spanish using `deletrear` and letter names.
  2. Names the days of the week and uses time-of-day expressions (*buenos días*).
  3. Counts from one to ten (and recognizes higher numbers) using `contar` and `número`.
  4. Reports a number (e.g. phone number) digit by digit in Spanish.
- **Linked roots:** `m1-nombre`, `m1-dia`, `m1-deletrear`, `m1-letra`, `m1-numero`,
  `m1-contar`, `m1-uno`

### Element 1.3 — Origin, country and nationality

- **Performance criteria**
  1. States country of residence and origin (*soy de Filipinas / vivo en Manila*) using
     `vivir` and `ser de`.
  2. States nationality using the correct adjective form (*mi nacionalidad es filipina*).
  3. Asks another person where they are from and where they live.
  4. Uses the definite article correctly with country/place nouns where required.
- **Linked roots:** `m1-pais`, `m1-nacion`, `m1-vivir`

### Element 1.4 — The immediate family and everyday actions

- **Performance criteria**
  1. Names immediate family members (*padre, madre, hermano, hijo*) with the correct
     definite article and possessive.
  2. Describes what family members do using regular present-tense verbs (*mi padre
     trabaja…*, *hablo tagalo e inglés*).
  3. States age using `tener` (*tengo veinte años*) and asks another's age.
  4. Names a friend and reports basic well-being (*mi amigo está bien*).
- **Linked roots:** `m1-familia`, `m1-padre`, `m1-madre`, `m1-hermano`, `m1-hijo`,
  `m1-trabajar`, `m1-hablar`, `m1-amigo`, `m1-edad`, `m1-bien`

### Element 1.5 — Physical descriptions and colors

- **Performance criteria**
  1. Describes a person's physical features using `tener` (*tengo el pelo negro y los
     ojos cafés*).
  2. Uses `ser` with adjectives of permanent quality (*mi padre es alto*).
  3. Names common colors and agrees them with the noun in gender and number (*el coche
     rojo / las plantas verdes*).
  4. Uses the definite article correctly with body-feature and color nouns.
- **Linked roots:** `m1-tener`, `m1-color`, `m1-alto`, `m1-pelo`, `m1-ojo`, `m1-rojo`,
  `m1-azul`, `m1-verde`, `m1-amarillo`, `m1-blanco`

### Element 1.6 — Basic courtesy expressions

- **Performance criteria**
  1. Thanks and responds to thanks (*muchas gracias / de nada*).
  2. Makes a polite request using *por favor*.
  3. Apologizes and asks for repetition (*perdón, ¿puede repetir, por favor?*).
  4. Uses *con permiso* to excuse self and *mucho gusto* on meeting someone.
- **Linked roots:** `m1-gracias`, `m1-favor`, `m1-perdon`, `m1-permiso`, `m1-gusto`

## Oral / Written Question Bank

Underpinning-knowledge questions with model answers. Each is tagged with its element code.

### Element 1.1

1. **[1.1]** When do you use `ser` and when do you use `estar` to say "I am"?
   *Model answer:* `ser` for identity, role, profession and inherent qualities (*soy
   estudiante*); `estar` for temporary states, feelings and location (*estoy bien*,
   *estoy en casa*).
2. **[1.1]** How do you ask someone their name and give your own?
   *Model answer:* *¿Cómo te llamas?* (informal) / *¿Cómo se llama usted?* (formal); answer
   *Me llamo…* — the verb is `llamarse`, a reflexive verb.
3. **[1.1]** How do you introduce a third person?
   *Model answer:* *Te presento a [name]* — using the verb `presentar`.
4. **[1.1]** Give a time-appropriate greeting for the morning and for the evening.
   *Model answer:* Morning *buenos días*; evening/night *buenas noches* (afternoon *buenas
   tardes*).

### Element 1.2

1. **[1.2]** What does `deletrear` mean and when would you use it?
   *Model answer:* "To spell"; used to spell a name or word letter by letter, e.g. *¿Cómo se
   deletrea tu apellido?*
2. **[1.2]** Count from one to five in Spanish.
   *Model answer:* uno, dos, tres, cuatro, cinco.
3. **[1.2]** How does `uno` change before a noun?
   *Model answer:* It becomes *un* before a masculine noun (*un hermano*) and *una* before a
   feminine noun (*una hermana*).
4. **[1.2]** Which verb means "to count," and give an example sentence.
   *Model answer:* `contar`; e.g. *Cuento del uno al diez en español.*

### Element 1.3

1. **[1.3]** How do you say where you live and where you are from?
   *Model answer:* *Vivo en…* (live, verb `vivir`) and *Soy de…* (origin, `ser de`).
2. **[1.3]** What is the difference between `país` and `nación / nacionalidad`?
   *Model answer:* `país` = country (the place); `nacionalidad` = nationality (the adjective
   describing a person, e.g. *filipina*).
3. **[1.3]** Why is `vivir` a regular `-ir` verb, and how is it conjugated for "I"?
   *Model answer:* It follows the standard `-ir` pattern; *yo vivo*.
4. **[1.3]** Make your nationality adjective agree: a Filipino man vs a Filipino woman.
   *Model answer:* *filipino* (m.) / *filipina* (f.).

### Element 1.4

1. **[1.4]** Which verb expresses age in Spanish, and how do you say "I am twenty"?
   *Model answer:* `tener` — *Tengo veinte años* (literally "I have twenty years"); never
   `ser` or `estar` for age.
2. **[1.4]** Conjugate `trabajar` (a regular `-ar` verb) for *yo* and *él/ella*.
   *Model answer:* *yo trabajo*, *él/ella trabaja*.
3. **[1.4]** Name three immediate family members with their definite articles.
   *Model answer:* *el padre, la madre, el hermano* (also *el hijo*, *la familia*).
4. **[1.4]** How do you ask someone their age?
   *Model answer:* *¿Qué edad tienes?* or *¿Cuántos años tienes?*

### Element 1.5

1. **[1.5]** Which verb describes physical features such as hair and eyes?
   *Model answer:* `tener` — *Tengo el pelo negro y los ojos cafés.*
2. **[1.5]** Why is it `ser alto` and not `estar alto`?
   *Model answer:* Height is an inherent/lasting quality, so it takes `ser` (*es alto*).
3. **[1.5]** How do colors agree with nouns? Give masculine and feminine examples.
   *Model answer:* Adjective agrees in gender and number: *el coche rojo* (m. sg.) / *la
   camisa roja* (f. sg.) / *las plantas verdes* (f. pl.). Colors ending in a consonant or
   *-e* (azul, verde) change only for number.
4. **[1.5]** Give the four definite articles and when each is used.
   *Model answer:* *el* (m. sg.), *la* (f. sg.), *los* (m. pl.), *las* (f. pl.) — e.g. *el
   pelo, la familia, los ojos, las letras*.

### Element 1.6

1. **[1.6]** How do you say "please" in Spanish?
   *Model answer:* *por favor* (from `favor`).
2. **[1.6]** What do you say to apologize and ask someone to repeat?
   *Model answer:* *Perdón, ¿puede repetir, por favor?*
3. **[1.6]** What is the difference between *perdón* and *con permiso*?
   *Model answer:* *Perdón* = sorry / excuse me (apology or to get attention); *con permiso* =
   excuse me when passing by or leaving (`permiso`).
4. **[1.6]** What do you say when meeting someone for the first time?
   *Model answer:* *Mucho gusto (en conocerte)* — "nice to meet you" (from `gusto`).

## Critical Aspects of Competency

The learner must demonstrate ALL of the following to be deemed competent in this unit:

- Greets, introduces self and others, and takes leave intelligibly in Spanish (Element 1.1).
- Correctly distinguishes and applies `ser` vs `estar` in personal-information contexts.
- Spells own name and counts to ten accurately in Spanish (Element 1.2).
- States origin, country and nationality with correct adjective agreement (Element 1.3).
- Names immediate family and conjugates regular present-tense verbs correctly (Element 1.4).
- Uses `tener` correctly for age and physical features (Elements 1.4 and 1.5).
- Describes a person physically with color/adjective agreement and the correct definite
  article (Element 1.5).
- Uses core courtesy expressions appropriately in interaction (Element 1.6).
- Sustains short, comprehensible spoken exchange at CEFR A1 without reverting to English for
  the target performances.

---

## Methods of Assessment

Competence in this unit is assessed through a combination of:

- **Demonstration / direct observation** — the learner performs the can-do tasks in the
  Demonstration / Observation Checklist (live, in class or supervised online), rated S / NY.
- **Oral questioning** — underpinning-knowledge questions from the Question Bank, focused on
  the key grammar (`ser`/`estar`, regular present, `tener`, definite articles).
- **Portfolio of Cinco Raíces "can-do" summaries** — the learner's running record of the 40
  Module 1 roots with self-authored example sentences evidencing each element.
- **Written task** — a short personal-introduction paragraph (name, origin, family, physical
  description, courtesy close) demonstrating Elements 1.1–1.6 in writing.

A learner is deemed competent only when satisfactory evidence is gathered across all six
elements and all critical aspects are met.

## Context of Assessment

- **Setting:** classroom or supervised online session.
- **Conditions:** real-time interaction with a qualified rater; the learner may use a
  vocabulary reference (Cinco Raíces portfolio) for the written task but performs oral tasks
  unaided.
- **Tools allowed:** Cinco Raíces root list / portfolio for written work; no machine
  translation during oral demonstration.
- **Language of instruction:** English (working language). Tagalog support is permitted for
  clarification only and is review-gated. `[FIL — pending G3 review]` Tagalog learner-facing
  rubric wording and glosses to be confirmed by the G3 review before learner release.
- **Language of performance:** Spanish is required for all target can-do performances.
