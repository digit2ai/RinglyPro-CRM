# Evidence Guide — Module 4: Places and Transportation

## Unit of Competency
**Navigate places, directions and transportation in Spanish.** (CEFR A2)

The learner can locate places relative to one another, name common modes of transport
and movement, describe what is happening at a transport hub, narrate a completed trip or
sightseeing experience, and both ask for and give street directions to others — performing
all of these orally and in writing in Spanish, drawing on the Module 4 "Cinco Raíces"
vocabulary roots (theme: *Lugares y Transporte*).

This guide is structured for instructional-designer review. It is competency-based and
audit-defensible: every assessment item maps to UoC → element → performance criterion →
Cinco Raíces root id. It makes **no legal-sufficiency claims**. Tagalog learner-facing
wording is review-gated and marked `[FIL — pending G3 review]`.

## Elements & Performance Criteria

### Element 4.1 — Directions and locations (directional prepositions)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. States where one place is in relation to another using directional/locational
     prepositions correctly (*cerca de, lejos de, enfrente de, detrás de, a la derecha de,
     a la izquierda de, entre, en la esquina de*).
  2. Names common urban reference points (*lugar, calle, esquina, cuadra*) and uses them
     to anchor a location.
  3. Combines a preposition + reference point in a complete sentence with *estar* for
     location (e.g. "El banco **está a la derecha de** la iglesia").
  4. Distinguishes *derecha* (right) from *izquierda* (left) without hesitation.
- **Linked roots:** `m4-lugar`, `m4-cerca`, `m4-lejos`, `m4-derecha`, `m4-izquierda`,
  `m4-enfrente`, `m4-detras`, `m4-esquina`, `m4-calle`, `m4-cuadra`

### Element 4.2 — Transport and movement verbs
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Names common modes of transport (*coche, autobús, tren*) and selects the correct
     one for a stated trip.
  2. Uses movement verbs in the present (*viajar, conducir*) with the preposition *en* for
     means of transport (e.g. "Vamos a Cavite **en coche**").
  3. Conjugates the irregular verb *conducir* correctly in the present (*conduzco*).
  4. Produces a complete sentence linking origin/destination + mode + verb
     (e.g. "Tomo el **autobús** para ir al trabajo").
- **Linked roots:** `m4-viajar`, `m4-coche`, `m4-autobus`, `m4-tren`, `m4-conducir`

### Element 4.3 — At the airport (present continuous)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Names airport/travel objects and stages (*aeropuerto, vuelo, maleta, billete*).
  2. Forms the present continuous correctly with *estar* + gerund (*estoy revisando,
     está comprando, están esperando*).
  3. Describes an action happening *right now* at the airport using the present continuous
     (e.g. "**Estamos esperando** en el aeropuerto ahora mismo").
  4. Uses *esperar* in context (waiting at a gate / counter) with present continuous.
- **Linked roots:** `m4-aeropuerto`, `m4-vuelo`, `m4-maleta`, `m4-billete`, `m4-esperar`

### Element 4.4 — City and tourist sights (past tense, regular & irregular)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Names city/tourist locations (*ciudad, museo, plaza, iglesia*).
  2. Narrates a completed visit using the regular preterite (*visité, entré, caminaron,
     recorrimos*).
  3. Narrates a completed trip/experience using irregular preterite verbs
     (*fui/fue [ir], hice/hizo [hacer], vi/vimos [ver], llegamos [llegar]*).
  4. Anchors the past event with a time marker (*ayer, la semana pasada, el año pasado,
     el sábado pasado*).
- **Linked roots:** `m4-visitar`, `m4-ciudad`, `m4-museo`, `m4-plaza`, `m4-iglesia`,
  `m4-ir`, `m4-hacer`, `m4-ver`, `m4-llegar`

### Element 4.5 — Asking and giving directions (imperative tú / usted)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Gives a sequence of street directions using imperatives (*siga/sigue, doble/dobla,
     cruce/cruza*) with directional language from 4.1.
  2. Selects the correct register: *tú* (informal) vs. *usted* (formal/courteous) and keeps
     it consistent throughout the exchange.
  3. Asks another person for directions politely (*preguntar*: "¿Cómo llego a…?",
     "pregunta a un policía…").
  4. Forms a negative imperative correctly (e.g. "**No se pierda**", "**No te pierdas**").
- **Linked roots:** `m4-seguir`, `m4-doblar`, `m4-cruzar`, `m4-preguntar`, `m4-perderse`

### Element 4.6 — Travel expressions
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Uses fixed travel/courtesy expressions appropriately (*Buen viaje; ida y vuelta;
     todo recto; al frente; hacia el centro*).
  2. Uses a travel aid noun correctly in context (*mapa*: "lleva un **mapa** por si te
     pierdes").
  3. Wishes a traveller well and gives a practical travel tip in one turn
     (e.g. "**Buen viaje**; lleva un mapa por si te pierdes").
  4. Combines a travel expression with a movement verb or direction from 4.2/4.5 in a
     natural utterance.
- **Linked roots:** `m4-mapa`, `m4-billete`, `m4-ir`, `m4-llegar`, `m4-perderse`,
  `m4-seguir`
  *(Note: element 4.6 is delivered jointly with 4.4 in Session 6; its evidence is drawn
  from the travel-expression usage embedded in the example sentences for these roots —
  e.g. "Buen viaje" (m4-mapa), "billete de ida y vuelta" (m4-billete), "tome el segundo
  tren hacia el centro" (m4-perderse).)*

## Oral / Written Question Bank
Underpinning-knowledge questions with model answers. Each is tagged with its element code.
Key grammar drawn from the Module 4 map: **directional prepositions · movement verbs ·
present continuous · past tense (reg/irreg) · imperative tú/usted**.

### Element 4.1 — directional prepositions
1. **[4.1]** Q: Which verb do you use to say where a place *is located*, and why not *ser*?
   A: *Estar* ("El banco **está** a la derecha"), because location of a place uses *estar*,
   not *ser*.
2. **[4.1]** Q: What preposition follows *cerca*, *lejos*, *enfrente* and *detrás* before a
   noun? A: *de* — *cerca **de** la estación*, *enfrente **del** mercado* (*de + el = del*).
3. **[4.1]** Q: Give the opposites of *cerca* and *a la derecha*. A: *lejos*; *a la
   izquierda*.
4. **[4.1]** Q: How would you say "between two shops"? A: *entre dos tiendas*.

### Element 4.2 — movement verbs
1. **[4.2]** Q: Which preposition expresses the means of transport? A: *en* — *en coche,
   en autobús, en tren, en avión* (idiomatically *a pie* for "on foot").
2. **[4.2]** Q: Conjugate *conducir* for *yo* in the present. Why is it irregular?
   A: *conduzco*; verbs ending in *-cir* take a *-zc-* in the first person singular.
3. **[4.2]** Q: Is *viajar* regular? Give the *yo* form. A: Yes, regular *-ar*; *viajo*.
4. **[4.2]** Q: Rephrase "I go to work by bus" in Spanish. A: *Voy al trabajo en autobús*
   (or *Tomo el autobús para ir al trabajo*).

### Element 4.3 — present continuous
1. **[4.3]** Q: How do you form the present continuous in Spanish? A: *estar* (conjugated)
   + the gerund (*-ando* for *-ar*, *-iendo* for *-er/-ir*) — e.g. *estoy esperando*,
   *está comprando*.
2. **[4.3]** Q: When do you use the present continuous? A: For an action in progress right
   now ("ahora mismo"), not for habitual actions.
3. **[4.3]** Q: Give the gerund of *esperar* and *comprar*. A: *esperando*, *comprando*.
4. **[4.3]** Q: Translate "The passengers are waiting at the boarding gate." A: *Los
   pasajeros **están esperando** en la puerta de embarque.*

### Element 4.4 — past tense (regular & irregular preterite)
1. **[4.4]** Q: What are the regular *-ar* preterite endings (yo, nosotros)? A: *-é*, *-amos*
   — *visité*, *visitamos*.
2. **[4.4]** Q: Give the preterite of *ir* and *ser* for *yo*. What is special? A: *fui* for
   both — *ir* and *ser* share the same preterite forms.
3. **[4.4]** Q: Preterite *yo* forms of *hacer* and *ver*? A: *hice*; *vi*.
4. **[4.4]** Q: Name three time markers that signal the preterite. A: *ayer, la semana
   pasada, el año pasado* (also *el sábado pasado*).

### Element 4.5 — imperative tú / usted
1. **[4.5]** Q: How does the affirmative *usted* imperative of *seguir* and *cruzar* look?
   A: *siga*, *cruce* (formal commands use the present-subjunctive form).
2. **[4.5]** Q: Give the *tú* command for *doblar* and *cruzar*. A: *dobla*, *cruza*
   (affirmative *tú* = third-person present indicative for regular verbs).
3. **[4.5]** Q: When do you choose *usted* over *tú*? A: For strangers, elders, formal or
   courteous situations (e.g. a tourist asking an official); *tú* for friends/peers.
4. **[4.5]** Q: Form the negative command "don't get lost" formally and informally.
   A: *No se pierda* (usted) / *No te pierdas* (tú).

### Element 4.6 — travel expressions
1. **[4.6]** Q: What do you say to wish someone a good trip? A: *¡Buen viaje!*
2. **[4.6]** Q: How do you ask for a round-trip ticket? A: *Un billete de ida y vuelta,
   por favor.*
3. **[4.6]** Q: Give two ways to say "straight ahead" / "right in front." A: *todo recto*;
   *al frente* (also *de frente*).
4. **[4.6]** Q: Combine a travel wish with a tip in one sentence. A: *Buen viaje; lleva un
   mapa por si te pierdes.*

## Critical Aspects of Competency
Overall competence is demonstrated when the learner can:
- Locate places relative to each other using directional prepositions + *estar* (4.1).
- Name modes of transport and use movement verbs with *en* for means (4.2).
- Form and use the present continuous (*estar* + gerund) to describe live actions (4.3).
- Narrate a completed trip/visit in the preterite, including the high-frequency irregulars
  *ir, hacer, ver, llegar* (4.4).
- **Both ask for and give** street directions, switching correctly between *tú* and *usted*
  registers and forming negative commands (4.5).
- Deploy fixed travel expressions naturally within a transaction or send-off (4.6).
- Sustain an A2-level travel/directions exchange in Spanish with comprehensible
  pronunciation and recover from a breakdown by asking for clarification.

---

## Methods of Assessment
Competence is assessed through a blend of:
- **Direct demonstration / observation** — role-plays (giving directions, airport check-in,
  buying a ticket) scored on the S/NY checklists above.
- **Oral questioning** — the underpinning-knowledge question bank, to confirm the learner
  understands *why* a form is used (e.g. *estar* vs *ser* for location, *tú* vs *usted*).
- **Portfolio of Cinco Raíces "can-do" summaries** — the learner's per-session self-recorded
  can-do statements mapped to the Module 4 roots (one summary per session 1–7).
- **Written task** — a short past-tense travel narrative (4–6 sentences using preterite +
  place/transport vocabulary) and a written set of directions from point A to point B.

---

## Context of Assessment
- **Where:** classroom or supervised online session.
- **Tools allowed:** a simple map / city diagram, picture prompts of airport and city
  scenes, and the role-play cue cards. Dictionaries and conjugation tables are **not**
  permitted during the oral demonstration.
- **Language of instruction:** English (working language of this guide); Tagalog scaffolding
  permitted for clarification only and is review-gated `[FIL — pending G3 review]`.
- **Language of target performance:** **Spanish is required** for all demonstration tasks
  and oral answers — the learner must perform the can-do in Spanish to be marked Satisfactory.
- **[FIL — pending G3 review]** Learner-facing task instructions may be glossed in Tagalog
  (e.g. *"Ituro mo ang daan papunta sa estasyon gamit ang 'usted'"*) but such glosses are
  pending G3 review and are not part of the assessed Spanish performance.
