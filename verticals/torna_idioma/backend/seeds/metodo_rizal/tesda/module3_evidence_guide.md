# Evidence Guide — Module 3: We Are What We Consume (Somos lo que consumimos)

## Unit of Competency
**Conduct everyday consumer and shopping transactions in Spanish.** (CEFR A2)

The learner is able to handle routine consumer interactions — describing a home and its
furnishings, shopping in stores and markets, choosing and buying clothing and food, asking
about and comparing prices and quantities, and asking/answering the questions that move a
transaction forward — using A2-level Spanish with the module's key grammar:
**ser/estar + adjective**, **direct- and indirect-object (DO/IO) pronouns**, **demonstratives
(este/ese/aquel)**, **gustar / preferir**, and **comparatives of equality (tan … como /
tanto(s) … como)**.

> Working language of this guide: English. Learner-facing target performance: Spanish.
> Tagalog learner-facing wording is review-gated and marked `[FIL — pending G3 review]`.
> This guide maps evidence to UoC → element → performance criterion. It makes **no
> legal-sufficiency claims**; it supports instructional-designer review only.

## Elements & Performance Criteria

### Element 3.1 — Home and furniture (ser/estar + adjective)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Names the parts of a home and at least four pieces of furniture in Spanish.
  2. Describes inherent qualities of a home/object with **ser + adjective** (e.g. *La casa
     es pequeña*), with correct gender/number agreement.
  3. Describes states/conditions of a home/object with **estar + adjective** (e.g. *La mesa
     está sucia*), choosing estar for changeable conditions.
  4. Contrasts ser and estar in the same description without confusing the two
     (e.g. *La silla es vieja pero está limpia*).
- **Linked roots:** `m3-casa`, `m3-mueble`, `m3-mesa`, `m3-silla`, `m3-habitacion`

### Element 3.2 — Shopping and stores (DO/IO pronouns)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Conducts a simple buy/sell exchange in a store or market using *comprar, vender,
     pagar*.
  2. Replaces a named object with a **direct-object pronoun** (lo/la/los/las), e.g.
     *Compro el cuaderno → Lo compro*.
  3. Uses an **indirect-object pronoun** (me/te/le/nos/les) to mark the recipient, e.g.
     *El vendedor me vende fruta*.
  4. Places object pronouns correctly before a conjugated verb (and recognizes attachment
     to infinitives).
- **Linked roots:** `m3-comprar`, `m3-tienda`, `m3-vender`, `m3-pagar`, `m3-mercado`

### Element 3.3 — Clothing (demonstratives)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Names common articles of clothing and the verb *llevar* (to wear).
  2. Selects the correct **demonstrative** by distance: **este/esta** (here), **ese/esa**
     (there, near listener), **aquel/aquella** (over there), with agreement.
  3. Points out and compares garments using demonstratives in context (e.g. *Esta camisa…,
     aquel vestido…*).
  4. States what clothing one is wearing or wants to wear with *llevar*.
- **Linked roots:** `m3-ropa`, `m3-camisa`, `m3-zapato`, `m3-vestido`, `m3-llevar`

### Element 3.4 — Food and restaurants (gustar / preferir)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Names foods, drinks, and the restaurant setting in Spanish.
  2. Expresses likes/dislikes with **gustar** using the correct IO pronoun and verb number
     (*me gusta* / *me gustan*; *a él no le gustan*).
  3. Expresses a choice between options with **preferir** (e→ie), e.g. *Prefiero el arroz*.
  4. Orders or chooses in a restaurant, combining gustar/preferir with food vocabulary.
- **Linked roots:** `m3-comida`, `m3-restaurante`, `m3-gustar`, `m3-preferir`, `m3-bebida`

### Element 3.5 — Quantities and prices (numbers, comparatives of equality)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. States and asks prices and quantities (precio, dinero, cantidad) using numbers.
  2. Compares two items as equal with **comparatives of equality**: **tan + adjective +
     como** (e.g. *tan barato como*) and **tanto/a/os/as + noun + como** (e.g. *tanto
     dinero como*).
  3. Distinguishes barato / caro and applies them in price comparisons.
  4. Handles amounts (medio kilo, una docena) and the currency term *peso(s)*.
- **Linked roots:** `m3-precio`, `m3-dinero`, `m3-barato`, `m3-caro`, `m3-cantidad`,
  `m3-costar`, `m3-pesos`, `m3-medio`, `m3-mas`

### Element 3.6 — Asking and answering questions (transaction dialogue)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Asks transaction questions with *preguntar, cuánto/cuántos, querer, necesitar*
     (e.g. *¿Cuánto cuesta?*, *¿Cuántos panes quieres?*).
  2. Answers a customer/seller appropriately with *responder, dar* and object pronouns
     (e.g. *Se lo digo*, *Aquí le doy el billete*).
  3. Sustains a short shopping dialogue: greet → ask price/quantity → choose → pay → get
     change, with politeness (*por favor*).
  4. Combines question words, object pronouns, and request verbs in a single coherent turn.
- **Linked roots:** `m3-preguntar`, `m3-responder`, `m3-querer`, `m3-necesitar`, `m3-dar`,
  `m3-cuanto`

## Oral / Written Question Bank
Underpinning-knowledge questions with model answers. Each is tagged with its element code.

### Element 3.1
- **[3.1] Q:** When do you use *ser* vs *estar* with an adjective?
  **A:** Use **ser** for inherent or defining qualities (size, shape, material, type): *La
  casa **es** pequeña*. Use **estar** for changeable states or conditions (clean/dirty,
  tidy/messy, location): *La mesa **está** sucia*.
- **[3.1] Q:** Why is *La habitación está ordenada* with estar, but *La habitación es
  luminosa* with ser?
  **A:** *Ordenada* (tidy) is a temporary condition → estar; *luminosa* (bright by design)
  is an inherent quality → ser.
- **[3.1] Q:** Make the adjective agree: *Los muebles … (nuevo).*
  **A:** *Los muebles **son nuevos*** — masculine plural agreement, ser for an inherent
  quality.
- **[3.1] Q:** Which verb describes where the table is located right now, ser or estar?
  **A:** **estar** — location is expressed with estar: *La mesa está en la cocina*.

### Element 3.2
- **[3.2] Q:** What is a direct-object pronoun and how do you replace *Compro el cuaderno*?
  **A:** A DO pronoun (lo/la/los/las) replaces the thing receiving the action: *Compro el
  cuaderno → **Lo** compro*.
- **[3.2] Q:** What does the *me* in *El vendedor me vende fruta* express?
  **A:** It is an **indirect-object pronoun** — the person *to/for whom* the action happens
  (the recipient/buyer).
- **[3.2] Q:** Where do object pronouns go with a conjugated verb?
  **A:** **Before** the conjugated verb: *Lo compro*, *Te pago*. (With an infinitive they
  may attach to the end: *quiero comprar**lo***.)
- **[3.2] Q:** Replace both objects: *Te pago el café.*
  **A:** ***Te lo** pago* — IO pronoun (te) before DO pronoun (lo).

### Element 3.3
- **[3.3] Q:** What is the difference between *este*, *ese*, and *aquel*?
  **A:** **este/esta** = this (near the speaker); **ese/esa** = that (near the listener);
  **aquel/aquella** = that over there (far from both).
- **[3.3] Q:** Why *aquellos zapatos* and not *esos zapatos* if the shoes are across the
  room?
  **A:** Distance from both speakers → **aquellos** (far). *Esos* would imply they are near
  the listener.
- **[3.3] Q:** Agree the demonstrative: *… (este) camisa.*
  **A:** ***Esta** camisa* — camisa is feminine singular.
- **[3.3] Q:** Which verb means "to wear" here, and give an example.
  **A:** **llevar**: *Hoy **llevo** esta chaqueta*.

### Element 3.4
- **[3.4] Q:** Why *me gusta la comida* but *me gustan las verduras*?
  **A:** With **gustar**, the verb agrees with the *thing liked*: singular thing → *gusta*;
  plural things → *gustan*. The *me* marks who likes it.
- **[3.4] Q:** How do you say "he doesn't like them"?
  **A:** *A él no **le gustan***. (IO pronoun *le* + plural *gustan* for the things.)
- **[3.4] Q:** How does *preferir* change in *yo* and *ella*?
  **A:** Stem change e→ie: *yo **prefiero***, *ella **prefiere*** (but *nosotros
  preferimos* keeps the e).
- **[3.4] Q:** Give a sentence choosing one dish over another.
  **A:** *Prefiero el arroz, pero ella prefiere el pan.*

### Element 3.5
- **[3.5] Q:** How do you say two things are equally cheap?
  **A:** **tan + adjective + como**: *Esta fruta es **tan barata como** la del mercado*.
- **[3.5] Q:** What's the difference between *tan … como* and *tanto … como*?
  **A:** **tan + adjective/adverb + como** compares qualities (*tan caro como*); **tanto/a/
  os/as + noun + como** compares quantities (*tanto dinero como*, *tantos productos como*),
  agreeing with the noun.
- **[3.5] Q:** Agree it: *Necesito … (tanto) cantidad de arroz que tú.*
  **A:** ***la misma** cantidad …* or, for equality with tanto: *tanta cantidad* — *tanto*
  agrees → **tanta** (cantidad is feminine singular).
- **[3.5] Q:** Ask how much a dress costs and answer that it equals another.
  **A:** *¿**Cuánto cuesta** este vestido? — Cuesta **tanto como** aquel.*

### Element 3.6
- **[3.6] Q:** What two question words ask "how much / how many," and how do they differ?
  **A:** **cuánto** (uncountable/price: *¿Cuánto cuesta?*) and **cuántos/cuántas**
  (countable, agreeing with the noun: *¿Cuántos panes?*).
- **[3.6] Q:** Answer a customer's price question using object pronouns.
  **A:** *Me pregunta el precio y **se lo** digo.* (se = le before lo; lo = the price.)
- **[3.6] Q:** How does *querer* conjugate in *yo*?
  **A:** e→ie stem change: *yo **quiero***. *Quiero esta blusa.*
- **[3.6] Q:** Give a polite request asking the seller to give you the change.
  **A:** *¿**Me da** el cambio, **por favor**?* (IO pronoun me + dar; politeness marker.)

## Critical Aspects of Competency
The learner must demonstrate **all** of the following for overall competence in this unit:
- Completes a realistic shopping/consumer transaction from greeting through payment and
  change, intelligibly and politely, in Spanish.
- Chooses **ser vs estar** correctly when describing homes, objects, and conditions
  (Element 3.1 grammar).
- Uses **direct- and indirect-object pronouns** correctly (form, gender/number, placement)
  to avoid repetition in a transaction (Element 3.2 grammar).
- Selects the correct **demonstrative by distance** with agreement when identifying or
  comparing goods (Element 3.3 grammar).
- Expresses preferences and likes/dislikes accurately with **gustar / preferir**, including
  verb–noun number agreement with gustar (Element 3.4 grammar).
- Forms at least one correct **comparative of equality** (tan…como / tanto…como) when
  comparing prices or quantities (Element 3.5 grammar).
- Asks and answers the core transaction questions (price, quantity, want/need) and responds
  appropriately (Element 3.6).
- Applies the **Cinco Raíces** vocabulary of the module (≥80% of the 35 roots recognized in
  context) when producing the above.

## Methods of Assessment
Competence is assessed through a combination of:
- **Demonstration / role-play** — a simulated shopping or consumer transaction (store,
  market, restaurant) observed against the checklist above; the rater plays seller or
  customer.
- **Oral questioning** — underpinning-knowledge questions from the bank (ser/estar, object
  pronouns, demonstratives, gustar/preferir, comparatives of equality) to confirm the
  learner understands *why*, not only *what*.
- **Portfolio of Cinco Raíces "can-do" summaries** — the learner's per-session self-record
  of the 7 root-clusters (35 roots) with one original example sentence each, evidencing
  vocabulary acquisition over the module.
- **Written task** — a short written shopping dialogue or a price/quantity comparison
  paragraph that exercises the key grammar in context.

Each method must trace to at least one element; no single method certifies the unit alone.

## Context of Assessment
- **Where:** A classroom or a supervised online session (proctored video for the oral/role-
  play components).
- **Conditions:** Realistic but low-stakes consumer scenarios (props, menus, price lists, or
  images of homes/garments/food may be provided as prompts).
- **Tools allowed:** Visual prompts, a printed menu/price list, and a vocabulary checklist
  for the *portfolio* component only — **not** during the oral demonstration of grammar.
- **Language of instruction / scaffolding:** English, with Tagalog support where helpful
  `[FIL — pending G3 review]`.
- **Language of target performance:** **Spanish** — the demonstration, role-play, and
  written tasks must be performed in Spanish at CEFR A2.
