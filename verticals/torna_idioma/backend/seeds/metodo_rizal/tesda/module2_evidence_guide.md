# Evidence Guide — Module 2: A Day in the Life

## Unit of Competency
Describe daily routines, schedules and plans in Spanish. (CEFR A1–A2)

This unit covers the learner's ability to talk about a typical day using reflexive verbs in the present, locate everyday actions in time (times of day, days, months, dates), state how often things happen using adverbs of frequency, and express near-future plans with *ir a* + infinitive. The "Cinco Raíces" (five-roots-per-session) method anchors all assessment items to the module's vocabulary root ids.

**Key grammar assessed:** reflexive verbs in the present tense; adverbs of time and frequency; near future (*ir a* + infinitive).

> Method note: this guide is a competency-based, audit-defensible mapping for instructional-designer review. It makes **no legal-sufficiency claims**. Tagalog learner-facing wording is review-gated and marked `[FIL — pending G3 review]`.

## Elements & Performance Criteria

### Element 2.1 — Daily routines and city places
- **Performance criteria**
  - PC2.1.1 Describes a personal morning routine in the present using at least four reflexive verbs conjugated correctly in the first person (e.g. *me despierto, me levanto, me ducho, me visto*).
  - PC2.1.2 Conjugates reflexive verbs across persons (yo / tú / él-ella) with the correct reflexive pronoun (me / te / se) and verb agreement.
  - PC2.1.3 Names at least four everyday places in the city and links each to an action of the daily routine (e.g. *trabajo en una oficina*, *espero el autobús en la parada*).
  - PC2.1.4 Pronounces reflexive forms and place nouns intelligibly to a sympathetic listener.
- **Linked roots:** m2-despertarse, m2-levantarse, m2-ducharse, m2-vestirse, m2-acostarse, m2-ciudad, m2-calle, m2-oficina, m2-mercado, m2-parada

### Element 2.2 — Schedules and times of day
- **Performance criteria**
  - PC2.2.1 Asks and answers *¿Qué hora es?* and gives the time correctly (using *es la* / *son las*, *de la mañana / tarde / noche*).
  - PC2.2.2 Locates routine actions within the parts of the day using *por la mañana / por la tarde / por la noche*.
  - PC2.2.3 Describes a personal schedule (*horario*) stating start and end times of at least two activities.
  - PC2.2.4 Uses *a las* + time to say when an action happens (e.g. *salgo a las siete*).
- **Linked roots:** m2-hora, m2-manana, m2-tarde, m2-noche, m2-horario

### Element 2.3 — Days, months and prepositions of time
- **Performance criteria**
  - PC2.3.1 Names the days of the week and months of the year and uses *el lunes / los lunes* correctly (one-time vs. habitual).
  - PC2.3.2 Uses prepositions of time correctly: *en* + month/year, *de … a …* for ranges (e.g. *de lunes a viernes*), *cada* + period.
  - PC2.3.3 Asks and answers *¿Cuál es la fecha de hoy?* and gives a date (day + *de* + month).
  - PC2.3.4 States the frequency of a weekly/monthly routine using a day or period correctly placed in the sentence.
- **Linked roots:** m2-semana, m2-lunes, m2-mes, m2-ano, m2-fecha

### Element 2.4 — Common actions in the present (regular and irregular)
- **Performance criteria**
  - PC2.4.1 Conjugates regular -ar, -er, -ir verbs in the present (e.g. *desayuno, como, estudio*) in the persons required by the task.
  - PC2.4.2 Conjugates the high-frequency irregular verbs *salir* and *volver* correctly (*salgo*, *vuelvo*).
  - PC2.4.3 Sequences three or more daily actions into a coherent narration of a typical day.
  - PC2.4.4 Distinguishes reflexive from non-reflexive present forms appropriately within the narration.
- **Linked roots:** m2-desayunar, m2-comer, m2-salir, m2-volver, m2-estudiar

### Element 2.5 — Frequency adverbs
- **Performance criteria**
  - PC2.5.1 Uses adverbs of frequency *siempre, nunca, a veces, con frecuencia, todos los días* with correct sentence placement.
  - PC2.5.2 Forms a correct negative-frequency sentence with *nunca* (e.g. *Nunca me acuesto antes de las nueve.*).
  - PC2.5.3 Answers a *¿Con qué frecuencia…?* question with an appropriate frequency adverb and a true statement about own routine.
  - PC2.5.4 Combines a frequency adverb with a reflexive verb and a time expression in one sentence.
- **Linked roots:** m2-siempre, m2-nunca, m2-aveces, m2-frecuencia, m2-todoslosdias

### Element 2.6 — Future plans (ir a + infinitivo)
- **Performance criteria**
  - PC2.6.1 Forms the near future *ir a* + infinitive correctly across persons (*voy a, vas a, va a, vamos a*).
  - PC2.6.2 States at least three near-future plans tied to time markers (*mañana, el fin de semana, la próxima semana*).
  - PC2.6.3 Expresses a wish/intention with *querer* + infinitive and links it to a plan (e.g. *Quiero aprender español porque voy a trabajar en el extranjero.*).
  - PC2.6.4 Asks a peer about their plans and responds to the same question truthfully.
- **Linked roots:** m2-ir, m2-plan, m2-finde, m2-proximo, m2-querer

## Oral / Written Question Bank

Underpinning-knowledge questions with model answers. Each question is tagged with its element code.

### Element 2.1
1. **[2.1] What is a reflexive verb, and how do you form the present tense of *despertarse* for *yo*?**
   *Model answer:* A reflexive verb shows the subject doing the action to itself; it needs a reflexive pronoun. For *yo* the pronoun is *me* and the verb agrees: *me despierto*. (Note *despertar* is stem-changing e→ie, so *despierto*.)
2. **[2.1] Why do we say *se levanta* and not *me levanta* when talking about another person?**
   *Model answer:* The reflexive pronoun must match the subject. For *él/ella* the pronoun is *se*, so *se levanta*; *me* is only for *yo*.
3. **[2.1] Name three places in the city and give a daily action you do at each.**
   *Model answer:* *Trabajo en una oficina; espero el autobús en la parada; mi madre va al mercado.* (Accept any true sentences using oficina, parada, mercado, calle, ciudad.)

### Element 2.2
1. **[2.2] How do you say "It is one o'clock" vs. "It is three o'clock" in Spanish, and why are they different?**
   *Model answer:* *Es la una* (singular *es la* for one) but *son las tres* (plural *son las* for two or more). The verb agrees with the number of hours.
2. **[2.2] What is the difference between *a las ocho* and *por la mañana*?**
   *Model answer:* *A las ocho* gives an exact clock time (when something happens); *por la mañana* gives a general part of the day. They can combine: *a las ocho por la mañana*.
3. **[2.2] How would you ask someone what time their class schedule (*horario*) starts?**
   *Model answer:* *¿A qué hora empieza tu horario de clases?* — answered e.g. *Empieza a las ocho.*

### Element 2.3
1. **[2.3] What is the difference between *el lunes* and *los lunes*?**
   *Model answer:* *El lunes* = this coming/specific Monday (one time); *los lunes* = every Monday (a habit).
2. **[2.3] Which preposition do you use with months and years? Give an example.**
   *Model answer:* Use *en*: *en el mes de mayo*, *en 2026*, *este año* for "this year."
3. **[2.3] How do you ask and answer for today's date?**
   *Model answer:* Ask *¿Cuál es la fecha de hoy?* Answer with day + *de* + month: *Es el doce de junio.*

### Element 2.4
1. **[2.4] How do you conjugate the irregular verb *salir* in the *yo* form, and how is it different from a regular -ir verb?**
   *Model answer:* *Salir* → *salgo* (irregular *yo* with -go). A regular -ir verb would end in -o without the g (e.g. *vivo*).
2. **[2.4] Give the *yo* present forms of *desayunar*, *comer* and *estudiar*.**
   *Model answer:* *desayuno, como, estudio.* (-ar → -o, -er → -o, -ar → -o.)
3. **[2.4] When do you NOT use a reflexive pronoun with these action verbs?**
   *Model answer:* When the action is not done to oneself: *como, estudio, salgo* take no reflexive pronoun, unlike *me ducho* or *me visto*.

### Element 2.5
1. **[2.5] Where do you place *siempre* and *nunca* in a sentence? Give examples.**
   *Model answer:* Usually before the verb: *Siempre desayuno…*, *Nunca me acuesto tarde.* *A veces* and *todos los días* can also start or end the sentence.
2. **[2.5] How does *nunca* affect the sentence — do you need a double negative?**
   *Model answer:* Before the verb, *nunca* alone is enough: *Nunca como carne.* If it comes after the verb, you add *no*: *No como carne nunca.*
3. **[2.5] How would you answer *¿Con qué frecuencia te despiertas temprano?*?**
   *Model answer:* With a frequency adverb + true statement: *Me despierto temprano todos los días / a veces / con frecuencia.*

### Element 2.6
1. **[2.6] How do you form the near future, and what are the *yo* and *nosotros* forms of *ir a*?**
   *Model answer:* *ir a* + infinitive. *Yo*: *voy a* + infinitive; *nosotros*: *vamos a* + infinitive (e.g. *vamos a empezar*).
2. **[2.6] Express a future plan for the weekend using a time marker.**
   *Model answer:* *El fin de semana voy a descansar con mi familia.* (Accept any *ir a* + infinitive with *el fin de semana / mañana / la próxima semana*.)
3. **[2.6] What is the difference between *quiero estudiar* and *voy a estudiar*?**
   *Model answer:* *Quiero estudiar* = "I want to study" (wish/intention, *querer* + infinitive); *voy a estudiar* = "I am going to study" (a planned near-future action).

## Critical Aspects of Competency
The learner must demonstrate ALL of the following for overall competence:
- Conjugates reflexive verbs in the present with the correct reflexive pronoun across at least the first and third persons (covers 2.1, links to 2.4–2.6 in narration).
- Tells the time and locates actions within the day, days, months and dates using correct time prepositions (2.2, 2.3).
- Conjugates regular and the high-frequency irregular present verbs (*salir, volver*) and sequences them into a coherent account of a typical day (2.4).
- Uses adverbs of frequency with correct placement and correct negation with *nunca* (2.5).
- Forms the near future *ir a* + infinitive correctly and states at least three plans tied to time markers (2.6).
- Sustains intelligible spoken/written Spanish at A1–A2 with a sympathetic interlocutor across all six elements.
- Each can-do is performed (not recited from memory) and traces to a Cinco Raíces root id.

## Methods of Assessment
- **Demonstration / observation:** learner performs the can-do tasks in the Demonstration Checklist (oral routine narration, time-telling, schedule presentation, peer interview). Rater records S / NY against the observable criteria.
- **Oral questioning:** underpinning-knowledge questions from the Question Bank, asked live to confirm the learner knows *why* (grammar rules for reflexives, time prepositions, frequency placement, near future) and not only *what*.
- **Portfolio of Cinco Raíces "can-do" summaries:** the learner's per-session five-roots logs (7 sessions × 5 roots = 35 roots for Module 2) with self-authored example sentences, evidencing vocabulary uptake mapped to root ids.
- **Written task:** a short written account of "Un día en mi vida" plus three near-future plans, demonstrating reflexive present, frequency adverbs, time/date prepositions and *ir a* + infinitive in connected text.

Assessment is holistic across the four methods; a single method alone is not sufficient for the unit.

## Context of Assessment
- **Where / conditions:** classroom or supervised online session (synchronous, camera/mic on for oral components).
- **Tools allowed:** the learner's Cinco Raíces vocabulary log and a clock/calendar prop for time/date tasks; no full-script reading aloud for the demonstration components (cues/notes only).
- **Language of instruction:** English (working language of this guide and rater notes); Tagalog support is permitted for clarification and is review-gated — `[FIL — pending G3 review]`.
- **Language of target performance:** Spanish is required for all can-do demonstration and the written task. Underpinning-knowledge questions may be answered in English where the rule explanation is the focus.
- **No legal-sufficiency claims:** this guide supports instructional assessment only; placement and recognition are config-driven and confirmed at program registration.
