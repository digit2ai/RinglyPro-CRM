> **Bilingual policy:** English is the working language of this guide. Learner-facing Tagalog wording is review-gated and marked `[FIL — pending G3 review]`. The Spanish *target text* always stays Spanish.

## Unit of Competency
**Demonstrate knowledge of José Rizal's life, works and legacy through Spanish-language texts.** (CEFR B1→B2)

Delivery and assessment are by **reading + translation of public-domain Spanish text or app-generated graded adaptations**, with **explanation in en/fil**. Source texts are limited to public-domain originals (*Noli Me Tángere* 1887, *El Filibusterismo* 1891, *Mi último adiós* 1896) and labeled graded adaptations — **no third-party copyrighted editions or translations**.

Successful assessment **emits evidence toward the "Rizal Studies — Completion Record."**

**Source-content keys** (from `rizal_sections.js`): `rz1`, `rz2`, `rz3`, `rz4`, `rz5`.
**Cinco Raíces engine** (the five-roots-a-night method, `roots_per_session: 5`, `annual_target_words: 1800`, SRS = SM-2) is the cross-cutting vocabulary backbone referenced in RZ.4 and applied to all elements as the learner builds a personal "can-do" root portfolio.

## Elements & Performance Criteria

### Element RZ.1 — Life & Historical Context
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Reads the B1 graded Spanish biography of Rizal aloud with intelligible pronunciation and natural sentence grouping.
  2. Translates the graded biography into English **and** explains it in Tagalog, preserving key facts (birth year 1861, Calamba, studies in Manila/Europe, multiple languages, wrote in Spanish to defend his people).
  3. States, in en/fil, the historical context: Spanish colonial Philippines and the reformist purpose of his writing.
  4. Extracts and glosses at least 5 key vocabulary roots from the passage (e.g. *nacer, estudiar, idioma, defender, despertar*) as personal Cinco Raíces entries.
- **Linked source / roots:** `rz1` (provenance: graded_adaptation, B1); Cinco Raíces method roots harvested from the passage.

### Element RZ.2 — Noli Me Tángere (graded excerpts)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Reads the **1887 public-domain opening** of *Noli Me Tángere* aloud and identifies it as the original-register text.
  2. Reads the graded adaptation and explains, in en/fil, how it simplifies the original (sentence length, vocabulary, register).
  3. Translates the graded adaptation into accurate English and gives a fil gloss of the gist (Capitán Tiago's dinner; everyone already talking about it).
  4. Compares original vs. adaptation: points to at least 2 concrete differences (e.g. *Reuníase / a finales de octubre*; the embedded clause flattened into two sentences).
  5. Identifies the work's purpose (exposing colonial abuses) in en/fil.
- **Linked source / roots:** `rz2` (public_domain_original 1887 + graded_adaptation, B1–B2); roots e.g. *reunirse, cena, anunciar, conversación, octubre*.

### Element RZ.3 — El Filibusterismo (graded excerpts)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Reads the **1891 public-domain opening** of *El Filibusterismo* aloud.
  2. Reads the graded adaptation and translates it into English (the steamer *Tabo* climbing the Pásig river toward La Laguna with many passengers).
  3. Explains, in en/fil, why the sequel is "darker and more political" than the *Noli*.
  4. Identifies B2-level features in the original (e.g. *penosamente, tortuoso curso*) and explains how the adaptation renders them (*despacio, por el río*).
  5. Locates the geographic references (río Pásig, La Laguna) and confirms comprehension in en/fil.
- **Linked source / roots:** `rz3` (public_domain_original 1891 + graded_adaptation, B2); roots e.g. *vapor, subir, río, pasajero, provincia*.

### Element RZ.4 — Rizal as Polyglot & the Five-Roots Method
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Reads the B1 meta-lesson passage and translates it into English (Rizal spoke many languages; each night he learned a few new words; patience → mastery).
  2. Explains, in en/fil, the connection between Rizal's reported nightly-roots habit and the app's **Cinco Raíces** engine (5 roots/night → ~1,800/year, SM-2 spacing).
  3. Demonstrates the method on a live passage: selects 5 roots, gives gloss_en + gloss_fil + a Spanish example sentence for each.
  4. Articulates the metacognitive principle ("poco a poco, con paciencia") in en/fil as a study strategy.
- **Linked source / roots:** `rz4` (graded_adaptation, B1); Cinco Raíces engine config (`roots_per_session: 5`, `annual_target_words: 1800`, `srs: SM-2`).

### Element RZ.5 — Legacy & National Significance
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Reads the **public-domain opening lines of *Mi último adiós* (1896)** aloud with attention to the poetic register.
  2. Translates the opening lines into English and gives a fil gloss, preserving imagery (*Patria adorada, perla del mar de oriente, perdido Edén*).
  3. States, in en/fil, the historical fact of Rizal's 1896 execution and his status as the Philippines' national hero.
  4. Explains why a farewell poem written in Spanish is significant to Filipino national identity (links back to RZ.1–RZ.3 themes).
  5. Distinguishes literal translation from poetic/figurative meaning for at least one line.
- **Linked source / roots:** `rz5` (public_domain_original 1896, B1–B2); roots e.g. *adiós, patria, región, perla, oriente*.

## Oral / Written Question Bank
*Underpinning-knowledge questions with model answers. Each is tagged with its element code.*

### RZ.1
1. **(RZ.1)** Where and when was Rizal born, according to the passage?
   *Model:* In Calamba, in 1861 ("José Rizal nació en Calamba en 1861").
2. **(RZ.1)** Why did Rizal write in Spanish?
   *Model:* To defend his people and awaken the consciousness of Filipinos ("Escribió en español para defender a su pueblo… despertó la conciencia de los filipinos").
3. **(RZ.1)** What does *despertó la conciencia* mean, and what is the root verb?
   *Model:* "He awakened the consciousness"; root verb *despertar* (to awaken). `[FIL — pending G3 review]` fil gloss: ginising ang kamalayan.
4. **(RZ.1)** Name two places where Rizal studied.
   *Model:* Manila and Europe ("Estudió en Manila y en Europa").

### RZ.2
1. **(RZ.2)** What event opens *Noli Me Tángere*?
   *Model:* A dinner (cena) given by don Santiago de los Santos, "Capitán Tiago," in late October.
2. **(RZ.2)** Give one difference between the 1887 original and the graded adaptation.
   *Model:* The original embeds a long subordinate clause ("Reuníase a fines de octubre… a dar una cena que…"); the adaptation splits it into two short sentences and uses *a finales de octubre*.
3. **(RZ.2)** What was the novel's purpose?
   *Model:* To expose the abuses of colonial society.
4. **(RZ.2)** Translate *ya el tema de todas las conversaciones.*
   *Model:* "already the topic of all conversations" — i.e., everyone was already talking about it.

### RZ.3
1. **(RZ.3)** What is the *Tabo*, and what is it doing in the opening of *El Filibusterismo*?
   *Model:* The *Tabo* is a steamer (vapor) slowly climbing the Pásig river toward the province of La Laguna with many passengers.
2. **(RZ.3)** What B2 vocabulary in the original does the adaptation simplify, and how?
   *Model:* *penosamente*→*despacio* (slowly); *el tortuoso curso del Pásig*→*por el río Pásig* (along the Pásig river).
3. **(RZ.3)** In what year was *El Filibusterismo* published, and how does it relate to the *Noli*?
   *Model:* 1891; it is the darker, more political sequel.
4. **(RZ.3)** What case/tense is *subía*, and what does it tell you?
   *Model:* Imperfect past (*subir*); it describes an ongoing past action — the boat was climbing the river.

### RZ.4
1. **(RZ.4)** How did Rizal reportedly learn languages, per the passage?
   *Model:* By learning a few new words each night ("Cada noche aprendía algunas palabras nuevas"), little by little with patience.
2. **(RZ.4)** How does this connect to the app's Cinco Raíces method?
   *Model:* The app uses five roots a night (~1,800 a year, SM-2 spaced repetition) — the same patient, incremental habit.
3. **(RZ.4)** What does *poco a poco, con paciencia* mean, and why does it matter for study?
   *Model:* "Little by little, with patience"; it frames consistent small daily effort as the path to mastery.
4. **(RZ.4)** Demonstrate: pick 5 roots from any RZ passage and gloss them.
   *Model:* Learner-produced; rater checks each has gloss_en + gloss_fil + an ES example sentence.

### RZ.5
1. **(RZ.5)** What is *Mi último adiós*, and when was it written?
   *Model:* Rizal's farewell poem, 1896 (public domain), written before his execution.
2. **(RZ.5)** Translate *perla del mar de oriente*, and explain its figurative meaning.
   *Model:* Literally "pearl of the sea of the orient"; figuratively, the Philippines as a precious land.
3. **(RZ.5)** Why is Rizal considered the Philippines' national hero?
   *Model:* He was executed in 1896 for his reformist writings; his Spanish-language works awakened national consciousness and made him a unifying figure.
4. **(RZ.5)** What is the difference between a literal and a poetic translation of *nuestro perdido Edén*?
   *Model:* Literal = "our lost Eden"; poetically it evokes a paradise/homeland that is being lost — emotional, not just factual.

## Critical Aspects of Competency
The learner must demonstrate **all** of the following for overall competence:
- Reads public-domain Spanish originals **and** graded adaptations aloud intelligibly, and **correctly distinguishes** original register from graded register.
- Produces **accurate English translations** of graded adaptations across all five elements (RZ.1–RZ.5).
- Explains content **in en/fil** without inventing facts beyond the source text or the documented historical record.
- Demonstrates the **Cinco Raíces five-roots method** live (5 roots with gloss_en + gloss_fil + ES example) and articulates its link to Rizal's reported habit.
- Distinguishes **literal vs. figurative meaning** in the poetic text (RZ.5).
- States Rizal's **biographical and historical significance** (born 1861, executed 1896, national hero) accurately.
- Cites correct **provenance** for each source (public-domain original vs. graded adaptation) — no third-party copyrighted material used.

## Methods of Assessment
Competence is assessed through a combination of:
- **Demonstration / observation** — reading aloud and live translation of the five RZ passages against the checklists above.
- **Oral questioning** — the underpinning-knowledge question bank (RZ.1–RZ.5), delivered en/fil with Spanish target text.
- **Portfolio of Cinco Raíces "can-do" summaries** — the learner's harvested roots (gloss_en + gloss_fil + ES example) and short en/fil self-explanations of each passage's gist.
- **Written task** — a written English translation of one graded adaptation plus a short en/fil note comparing an original to its adaptation (RZ.2 or RZ.3).

All assessment outputs **emit evidence toward the "Rizal Studies — Completion Record."** This guide asserts **no legal-sufficiency**; equivalence/recognition is determined by TESDA and the accredited assessor.

## Context of Assessment
- **Setting:** Classroom or supervised online session.
- **Tools allowed:** Source passages from `rizal_sections.js` (public-domain originals + graded adaptations); the learner's Cinco Raíces root portfolio; a Spanish↔en/fil dictionary where the rubric permits (translation-with-aid vs. unaided is set by the assessor).
- **Language of instruction / explanation:** English and Tagalog (en/fil); **Tagalog learner-facing wording is review-gated** `[FIL — pending G3 review]`.
- **Target-performance language:** **Spanish is required** for the reading-aloud and source-comprehension tasks; the Spanish target text is never replaced.
- **Provenance constraint:** only public-domain Spanish originals (1887 / 1891 / 1896) and labeled app-generated graded adaptations may be used.
- **Placement:** config-driven, confirmed at program registration.
