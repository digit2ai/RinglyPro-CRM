## Unit of Competency

**Engage with contemporary culture and society in Spanish** (CEFR B2).

The learner can describe and discuss contemporary cultural domains — art, music, cinema and
television, fashion, gastronomy, and customs and celebrations — and can debate questions of
society, identity, diversity, influence, and cultural heritage. At B2 the learner produces
clear, detailed descriptions and arguments; expresses, justifies, and defends opinions about
present and future cultural trends; recounts present and past cultural experiences; and uses
descriptive adjectives/adverbs, opinion expressions, and adverbs of time and frequency
accurately and with appropriate register.

**Key grammar (this module):** descriptive adjectives and adverbs · present-tense expression of
tastes (`gustar`/`encantar`/`fascinar`/`interesar`/`apasionar` family with indirect-object
pronouns) · opinion expressions (`creo que` / `pienso que` / `me parece que` / `en mi opinión` /
`considero que` / `estoy convencido de que`) · present and future opinion (present + future-tense
forecasting: `seguirá`, `serán`, `ganará`, `volverá`, `veremos`) · present and past experiences
(present perfect `he/hemos + participio` vs. preterite/imperfect for recounting) · adverbs of
time and frequency (`tradicionalmente`, `normalmente`, `a menudo`, `rara vez`, `generalmente`,
`a veces`, `antiguamente`, `hoy en día`, `frecuentemente`, `siempre`, `cada año`).

## Elements & Performance Criteria

For each element the **Linked roots** are the `id` values from `module12_vocab_roots.json` that
provide the lexical evidence base. Session 7 (integration, `m12-identidad`, `m12-sociedad`,
`m12-diversidad`, `m12-influencia`, `m12-patrimonio`) is distributed across Elements 12.1 and
12.6 per the source `element: "12.1 / 12.6"` tag.

### Element 12.1 — Art and cultural expressions (descriptive adjectives and adverbs)

- **Performance criteria**
  1. Describes works of art (murals, sculpture, contemporary pieces) in Spanish using
     descriptive adjectives correctly inflected for gender and number (e.g.
     *una escultura abstracta delicada*, *obras vanguardistas*).
  2. Modifies descriptions with adverbs of degree/manner (e.g.
     *profundamente vanguardista*, *enormemente colorido*, *cuidadosamente iluminada*).
  3. Compares a traditional and a contemporary cultural expression, justifying the contrast.
  4. Discusses cultural identity and heritage as expressed through art, using `m12-identidad`,
     `m12-diversidad`, and `m12-patrimonio` in connected discourse.
  5. Sustains a coherent oral or written description of a gallery, exhibition, or public artwork
     of at least six sentences at B2 fluency.
- **Linked roots:** `m12-vanguardia`, `m12-mural`, `m12-escultura`, `m12-contemporaneo`,
  `m12-galeria`, `m12-identidad`, `m12-diversidad`, `m12-patrimonio`.

### Element 12.2 — Music and genres (expressing present-tense tastes)

- **Performance criteria**
  1. States musical tastes in the present tense using the `gustar` family with the correct
     indirect-object pronoun (*me encanta*, *no me gustan nada*, *nos gusta*, *les apasionan*).
  2. Distinguishes likes and dislikes and grades intensity (*me fascinan*, *me interesan mucho*,
     *no me gustan nada*).
  3. Names and characterizes at least three musical genres/elements (género, melodía, letra,
     ritmo, concierto) with appropriate descriptive adjectives.
  4. Explains *why* a genre or piece is liked, linking taste to a reason (*porque nos da ganas
     de bailar*).
  5. Maintains subject–verb agreement across singular/plural triggers (*me gusta el ritmo* vs.
     *me gustan las melodías*).
- **Linked roots:** `m12-genero`, `m12-melodia`, `m12-letra`, `m12-ritmo`, `m12-concierto`.

### Element 12.3 — Cinema and television (opinion expressions)

- **Performance criteria**
  1. Expresses opinions about films and series using a range of opinion frames
     (*en mi opinión*, *me parece que*, *considero que*, *pienso que*, *estoy convencido de que*).
  2. Evaluates specific film elements (guion, actuación, película, serie, documental) with
     justified judgments (*el guion está muy bien escrito porque los diálogos son realistas*).
  3. Concedes a counterpoint within an opinion (*es entretenida, aunque creo que el final es un
     poco flojo*).
  4. Supports an opinion with at least one reason or example.
  5. Uses correct indicative mood after affirmative opinion verbs (*creo que es…*,
     *pienso que la actuación es…*).
- **Linked roots:** `m12-pelicula`, `m12-serie`, `m12-guion`, `m12-actuacion`, `m12-documental`.

### Element 12.4 — Fashion and trends (present and future opinion)

- **Performance criteria**
  1. States a present-tense opinion about a current fashion trend (*creo que la moda actual
     valora lo cómodo*).
  2. Forecasts a future development using the simple future or `ir a + infinitivo`
     (*esa tendencia seguirá creciendo*, *las marcas serán cada vez más importantes*).
  3. Links a present observation to a future prediction in one coherent statement.
  4. Describes garments, designs, brands, and trends (prenda, diseño, marca, tendencia, moda)
     with relevant adjectives.
  5. Defends a prediction with a plausible reason or social context.
- **Linked roots:** `m12-moda`, `m12-tendencia`, `m12-diseno`, `m12-marca`, `m12-prenda`.

### Element 12.5 — Gastronomy (present and past experiences)

- **Performance criteria**
  1. Recounts a past culinary experience using the preterite/imperfect
     (*el año pasado degustamos…*, *mi abuela me enseñó esta receta cuando era niña*).
  2. Reports lifetime/recent experience using the present perfect (*he explorado la
     gastronomía…*, *nunca he probado…*, *hemos disfrutado…*).
  3. Contrasts a past habit with a present practice (*me enseñó… y todavía la preparo cada
     domingo*).
  4. Describes flavors, dishes, recipes, and fusion cuisine (sabor, receta, gastronomía,
     fusión, degustar) with sensory adjectives.
  5. Selects the correct past aspect (present perfect vs. preterite) for the time frame given.
- **Linked roots:** `m12-gastronomia`, `m12-sabor`, `m12-receta`, `m12-fusion`, `m12-degustar`.

### Element 12.6 — Customs and celebrations (adverbs of time and frequency)

- **Performance criteria**
  1. Describes a custom, festival, tradition, or rite (costumbre, celebración, fiesta,
     tradición, rito) in Spanish.
  2. Uses adverbs of frequency accurately (*siempre*, *normalmente*, *a menudo*, *a veces*,
     *rara vez*, *generalmente*, *frecuentemente*).
  3. Uses adverbs of time to place customs across eras (*tradicionalmente*, *antiguamente*,
     *hoy en día*, *cada año*).
  4. Contrasts how a custom was practiced in the past with how it is practiced now
     (*antiguamente… pero hoy en día apenas se practica*).
  5. Connects customs to broader society and contemporary debate, drawing on `m12-sociedad` and
     `m12-influencia` (*la influencia de las redes sociales en la cultura juvenil…*).
- **Linked roots:** `m12-costumbre`, `m12-celebracion`, `m12-fiesta`, `m12-tradicion`,
  `m12-rito`, `m12-sociedad`, `m12-influencia`.

## Oral / Written Question Bank

Underpinning-knowledge questions with model answers. Each is tagged with its element code. These
probe the **key grammar** of the module, not just recall.

### Element 12.1

- **[12.1-Q1]** When do adjectives change form in Spanish, and how would you describe *una
  escultura*?
  *Model:* Adjectives agree in gender and number with the noun. *Escultura* is feminine
  singular, so adjectives take feminine singular endings: *una escultura abstracta, delicada,
  contemporánea.*
- **[12.1-Q2]** What is the difference between an adjective and an adverb in *“una obra
  profundamente vanguardista”*?
  *Model:* *Vanguardista* is an adjective describing the noun *obra*; *profundamente* is an
  adverb of degree modifying the adjective, intensifying how avant-garde the work is.
- **[12.1-Q3]** How do you form an adverb of manner from an adjective? Give one from this
  element.
  *Model:* Take the feminine singular adjective and add *-mente*: *cuidadosa → cuidadosamente*
  (*cuidadosamente iluminada*).
- **[12.1-Q4]** Why might we use *patrimonio* and *identidad* when discussing art?
  *Model:* Art expresses cultural *identidad* and forms part of a community's *patrimonio*
  (heritage) that must be protected — they connect the artwork to society at B2 level.

### Element 12.2

- **[12.2-Q1]** With verbs like *gustar* and *encantar*, what agrees with what?
  *Model:* The verb agrees with the **thing liked** (the grammatical subject), not the person.
  *Me gusta el ritmo* (singular) vs. *me gustan las melodías* (plural). The person is the
  indirect object: *me, te, le, nos, les.*
- **[12.2-Q2]** How do you say you strongly dislike something?
  *Model:* Add *nada*: *no me gustan nada los géneros demasiado ruidosos.*
- **[12.2-Q3]** Give two verbs besides *gustar* for expressing tastes and use one.
  *Model:* *encantar, fascinar, interesar, apasionar.* E.g. *me fascinan las melodías suaves*;
  *les apasionan los conciertos en vivo.*
- **[12.2-Q4]** How do you justify a taste? Give an example with *porque*.
  *Model:* *Nos gusta el ritmo de la música latina porque nos da ganas de bailar.*

### Element 12.3

- **[12.3-Q1]** Name three ways to introduce an opinion in Spanish.
  *Model:* *En mi opinión…, me parece que…, creo/pienso que…, considero que…, estoy convencido
  de que…*
- **[12.3-Q2]** After *creo que* in the affirmative, do you use indicative or subjunctive?
  *Model:* Indicative — *creo que la actuación es brillante.* (Subjunctive appears with the
  negative, *no creo que sea…*, which is beyond this affirmative-opinion task.)
- **[12.3-Q3]** How do you add a counterpoint to an opinion? Give an example.
  *Model:* Use *aunque* or *pero*: *Me parece que la serie es entretenida, aunque creo que el
  final es un poco flojo.*
- **[12.3-Q4]** Evaluate a film's script (*guion*) with a justified opinion.
  *Model:* *Considero que el guion está muy bien escrito porque los diálogos son realistas.*

### Element 12.4

- **[12.4-Q1]** How do you form the simple future, and how would you predict a trend?
  *Model:* Add future endings (*-é, -ás, -á, -emos, -éis, -án*) to the infinitive:
  *seguir → seguirá*, *ser → serán*, *ganar → ganará.* E.g. *esa tendencia seguirá creciendo.*
- **[12.4-Q2]** What is another way to express the future besides the simple future?
  *Model:* *ir a + infinitivo*: *la moda va a cambiar.* It often signals nearer or more
  intended future.
- **[12.4-Q3]** Link a present opinion to a future forecast in one sentence.
  *Model:* *Creo que la moda actual valora lo cómodo, y opino que esa tendencia seguirá
  creciendo.*
- **[12.4-Q4]** How do you justify a prediction about brands?
  *Model:* *Opino que las marcas sostenibles serán cada vez más importantes para los jóvenes*
  (justification = the values of younger consumers).

### Element 12.5

- **[12.5-Q1]** When do you use the present perfect vs. the preterite for experiences?
  *Model:* Present perfect (*he/hemos + participio*) for lifetime/recent experience with no
  closed time (*he explorado la gastronomía toda mi vida*, *nunca he probado…*). Preterite for a
  finished, dated event (*el año pasado degustamos…*).
- **[12.5-Q2]** Form the present perfect of *probar* in the first person.
  *Model:* *he probado* (auxiliary *haber* + past participle *probado*). Negative experience:
  *nunca he probado.*
- **[12.5-Q3]** Why is the imperfect used in *“cuando era niña”*?
  *Model:* The imperfect describes background, age, and habitual past states/actions, not a
  single completed event — *me enseñó esta receta cuando era niña.*
- **[12.5-Q4]** Contrast a past habit with a present one in a single sentence.
  *Model:* *Mi abuela me enseñó esta receta cuando era niña y todavía la preparo cada domingo.*

### Element 12.6

- **[12.6-Q1]** List four adverbs of frequency, from most to least frequent.
  *Model:* *siempre > normalmente/generalmente > a menudo/frecuentemente > a veces > rara vez.*
- **[12.6-Q2]** Which adverbs locate a custom in past vs. present time?
  *Model:* Past: *antiguamente, tradicionalmente.* Present: *hoy en día, actualmente.*
- **[12.6-Q3]** Contrast how a rite was practiced before and now.
  *Model:* *Antiguamente, este rito se repetía cada noche, pero hoy en día apenas se practica.*
  (imperfect for past habit; present for the current situation).
- **[12.6-Q4]** How do you connect a custom to society or media influence at B2?
  *Model:* *La influencia de las redes sociales en la cultura juvenil cambia muchas de nuestras
  costumbres hoy en día* — links `costumbre`/`influencia`/`sociedad`.

## Critical Aspects of Competency

To be judged **competent overall**, the learner must demonstrate ALL of the following:

- Describes at least one contemporary cultural domain in Spanish with correctly agreed
  descriptive **adjectives** and at least one **adverb** of degree or manner (12.1).
- Expresses present-tense **tastes** with the *gustar* family using the correct indirect-object
  pronoun and subject–verb agreement (12.2).
- Produces **opinions** with at least two distinct opinion frames and correct indicative mood,
  including one justification (12.3).
- Connects a **present** observation to a **future** forecast using the simple future or
  *ir a + infinitivo* (12.4).
- Recounts **past** and **present** experiences, selecting present perfect vs. preterite/
  imperfect correctly for the time frame (12.5).
- Uses **adverbs of time and frequency** accurately and contrasts a past vs. present practice
  (12.6).
- Sustains intelligible, coherent communication at CEFR **B2** across at least four of the six
  elements, with errors that do not impede understanding.
- Draws on the Module 12 **Cinco Raíces** roots appropriately and in context (not as isolated
  recall).

## Methods of Assessment

Competence is assessed through a combination of:

- **Demonstration / direct observation** — the learner performs the can-do tasks in the
  Demonstration / Observation Checklist (oral description, opinion, forecast, experience
  narration) in Spanish, rated S / NY against observable criteria.
- **Oral questioning** — the rater poses underpinning-knowledge questions from the Question Bank
  to confirm the learner understands the key grammar (adjective/adverb agreement, *gustar*
  syntax, opinion frames, future formation, perfect vs. preterite, frequency adverbs).
- **Portfolio of Cinco Raíces "can-do" summaries** — the learner's session-by-session self-
  evidenced "can-do" entries for the 35 Module 12 roots across the 7 sessions, showing
  productive use in context.
- **Written task** — a short B2 composition (e.g. a review of a film/exhibition, a forecast of a
  fashion trend, or a description of a celebration) integrating descriptive adjectives/adverbs,
  opinion expressions, and time/frequency adverbs as relevant to the prompt.

Assessment evidence may be combined across methods; a single integrated task (e.g. a recorded
cultural-topic monologue + follow-up Q&A) can evidence several elements at once. This guide
makes **no legal-sufficiency claims** regarding TESDA certification outcomes.

- **Tools allowed:** the prompt/topic card and a glossary of Module 12 roots may be available for
  written tasks at the assessor's discretion; spoken demonstration is unaided beyond the topic
  prompt.
- **Language of instruction / mediation:** English; Tagalog/Filipino may be used for clarification
  and learner support. `[FIL — pending G3 review]` Filipino mediation script and learner-facing

- **Language of target performance:** **Spanish is required** for all assessed can-do
  performances. Underpinning-knowledge answers may be given in English or Filipino, but the
  Spanish examples produced must be in Spanish.
- **Level:** CEFR B2. Errors are acceptable where they do not impede communication.
