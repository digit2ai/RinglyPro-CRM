## Unit of Competency

**UoC:** *Discuss culture, traditions and the arts in Spanish.* (CEFR B1вҖ“B2)

The learner can sustain extended spoken and written discourse about Filipino and Hispanic
festivities, the arts, literature, gastronomy, social protocol and folklore; can compare and
evaluate cultural phenomena; can narrate cultural events across past and present time frames;
and can express and justify opinions using appropriate register and courtesy. Element **8.3
(Literature and writers)** is the designated **Rizal literature anchor** for this module and
feeds evidence toward the cross-cutting Rizal Studies unit.

**Module key grammar (from the TESDA Competency Map):**

- Present perfect vs. preterite (8.1)
- Comparatives and superlatives (8.1)
- Present subjunctive for opinion (8.2)
- Narrative connectors and past tenses (8.3 вҖ” Rizal anchor)
- Descriptive adjectives and adverbs (8.4)
- Courtesy forms of address (usted / formal register) (8.5)
- Present / past contrast (8.6)

## Elements & Performance Criteria

### Element 8.1 вҖ” Festivities and celebrations

- **Performance criteria**
  - PC8.1.a вҖ” Describes a festivity or celebration the learner has attended, using **present
    perfect** for life experience ("he asistido a mГЎs festividades que nunca").
  - PC8.1.b вҖ” Narrates a past edition of a festival using **preterite** for completed events
    ("el aГұo pasado celebramos la fiesta del pueblo").
  - PC8.1.c вҖ” **Compares** two festivities using comparatives of inequality/equality
    ("la feria de CebГә es mucho mГЎs concurrida que la de nuestro barrio").
  - PC8.1.d вҖ” Identifies the **most/least** notable element of a celebration using
    **superlatives** ("la procesiГіn mГЎs solemne de todas").
  - PC8.1.e вҖ” Names and explains at least four festivity-related vocabulary items in context.
- **Linked roots:** `m8-festividad`, `m8-celebrar`, `m8-procesion`, `m8-feria`,
  `m8-fuegos-artificiales`, `m8-tradicion`, `m8-fiesta`, `m8-carroza`, `m8-disfraz`,
  `m8-conmemorar`

### Element 8.2 вҖ” Art and cultural expression

- **Performance criteria**
  - PC8.2.a вҖ” Expresses a personal opinion about a work of art using **present subjunctive**
    after expressions of evaluation/emotion ("me parece increГӯble que el arte filipino seaвҖҰ").
  - PC8.2.b вҖ” Expresses doubt or disagreement using subjunctive after verbs of
    doubt/negation ("no creo que esa pintura sea de Juan Luna").
  - PC8.2.c вҖ” Makes a recommendation about visiting/experiencing art using subjunctive
    ("recomiendo que visites el museo").
  - PC8.2.d вҖ” Distinguishes and uses arts vocabulary (painting, sculpture, museum, work,
    art) accurately in context.
- **Linked roots:** `m8-arte`, `m8-pintura`, `m8-escultura`, `m8-museo`, `m8-obra`

### Element 8.3 вҖ” Literature and writers *(Rizal literature anchor)*

- **Performance criteria**
  - PC8.3.a вҖ” Sequences events in a literary biography using **narrative connectors**
    ("primeroвҖҰ despuГ©sвҖҰ mientrasвҖҰ aunqueвҖҰ sin embargo").
  - PC8.3.b вҖ” Narrates Rizal's life and works using **past tenses** in contrast (preterite for
    completed acts; imperfect for background: "estudiГі en EuropaвҖҰ mientras vivГӯa lejos").
  - PC8.3.c вҖ” Identifies and discusses literary terms (literature, writer, novel, poem,
    character) in Spanish.
  - PC8.3.d вҖ” Relates Rizal's two novels and at least one poem to their context, and links a
    fictional **character** to the author's own life ("aunque el personajeвҖҰ sin embargo
    refleja la vida del propio Rizal").
  - PC8.3.e вҖ” Produces a short coherent narrative paragraph about a writer using connected
    discourse rather than isolated sentences.
- **Linked roots:** `m8-literatura`, `m8-escritor`, `m8-novela`, `m8-poema`, `m8-personaje`

### Element 8.4 вҖ” Gastronomy

- **Performance criteria**
  - PC8.4.a вҖ” Describes a dish using **descriptive adjectives** of taste/texture ("crujiente
    por fuera y jugoso por dentro").
  - PC8.4.b вҖ” Qualifies descriptions with **adverbs** of manner/degree ("deliciosamente
    variada", "suavemente dulce", "cuidadosamente").
  - PC8.4.c вҖ” Explains how a dish is prepared or served, following or referencing a recipe.
  - PC8.4.d вҖ” Discusses flavor profiles (sweet, sour, savory) and uses gastronomy vocabulary
    accurately in context.
- **Linked roots:** `m8-gastronomia`, `m8-sabor`, `m8-platillo`, `m8-receta`, `m8-dulce`

### Element 8.5 вҖ” Social customs and protocol

- **Performance criteria**
  - PC8.5.a вҖ” Addresses an interlocutor using the **courtesy form usted** with correct verb
    agreement ("le pido por respeto que se siente, don Manuel").
  - PC8.5.b вҖ” Uses formal greetings and titles appropriately (don/doГұa, seГұor/seГұora,
    director/directora) in a respectful exchange.
  - PC8.5.c вҖ” Explains a social custom and the respect/courtesy expectation behind it
    ("por costumbre, los mГЎs jГіvenes saludan primero a los mayores").
  - PC8.5.d вҖ” Performs the role of a courteous host (anfitriГіn), making guests welcome with
    appropriate register.
- **Linked roots:** `m8-costumbre`, `m8-respeto`, `m8-cortesia`, `m8-saludo`, `m8-anfitrion`

### Element 8.6 вҖ” Folklore and legends

- **Performance criteria**
  - PC8.6.a вҖ” Contrasts **present and past** when discussing folklore ("antes los mitos
    explicaban la naturaleza; ahora la ciencia ofrece otras respuestas").
  - PC8.6.b вҖ” Retells a legend or tale, using past tenses for the narrative and present tense
    for its living significance ("segГәn la leyendaвҖҰ aГәn hoy mucha gente la cree").
  - PC8.6.c вҖ” Distinguishes folklore, legend, myth and tale, and explains the role of oral
    tradition in transmitting culture.
  - PC8.6.d вҖ” Connects a folkloric story to its cultural meaning or value for the community.
- **Linked roots:** `m8-folclore`, `m8-leyenda`, `m8-mito`, `m8-cuento`, `m8-tradicion-oral`

## Oral / Written Question Bank

Underpinning-knowledge questions with model answers. Each question is tagged with its element code.

### Element 8.1

- **Q8.1-1 [8.1]** вҖ” When do you use the present perfect instead of the preterite when talking
  about festivities?
  **Model answer:** Use the present perfect (he/has + participle) for experiences within a time
  frame still open or connected to now ("este aГұo he asistido aвҖҰ"); use the preterite for events
  finished in a closed past time ("el aГұo pasado celebramosвҖҰ").
- **Q8.1-2 [8.1]** вҖ” How do you form a comparison of inequality in Spanish? Give one example
  about a festival.
  **Model answer:** mГЎs/menos + adjective + que. Example: "La feria de CebГә es **mГЎs
  concurrida que** la de nuestro barrio."
- **Q8.1-3 [8.1]** вҖ” How do you form a superlative? Give an example with "procesiГіn".
  **Model answer:** el/la + mГЎs/menos + adjective (+ de). Example: "La procesiГіn **mГЎs
  solemne de** todas."
- **Q8.1-4 [8.1]** вҖ” Name three things you might see at a fiesta and say each in Spanish.
  **Model answer:** carroza (float), disfraz (costume), fuegos artificiales (fireworks),
  procesiГіn, feria.

### Element 8.2

- **Q8.2-1 [8.2]** вҖ” Why is the subjunctive used in "Me parece increГӯble que el arte filipino
  **sea** tan poco conocido"?
  **Model answer:** Expressions of personal evaluation/emotion ("me parece increГӯble que")
  trigger the subjunctive in the subordinate clause.
- **Q8.2-2 [8.2]** вҖ” How does the verb change when you doubt or deny something about art?
  **Model answer:** After doubt/negation (no creo que, dudo que) the verb goes to subjunctive:
  "No creo que esa pintura **sea** de Juan Luna."
- **Q8.2-3 [8.2]** вҖ” Give a sentence recommending a museum visit using the subjunctive.
  **Model answer:** "Recomiendo que **visites** el museo nacional antes de que cierre la
  exposiciГіn."

### Element 8.3 (Rizal anchor)

- **Q8.3-1 [8.3]** вҖ” Which narrative connectors can you use to sequence and contrast events
  in a story?
  **Model answer:** Sequence: primero, despuГ©s, luego, mientras, finalmente. Contrast:
  aunque, sin embargo, pero, no obstante.
- **Q8.3-2 [8.3]** вҖ” When telling Rizal's life, when do you use preterite vs. imperfect?
  **Model answer:** Preterite for completed, specific actions ("estudiГі en Europa", "escribiГі
  Noli Me TГЎngere"); imperfect for background, ongoing or habitual states ("mientras
  **vivГӯa** lejos de su patria").
- **Q8.3-3 [8.3]** вҖ” Name Rizal's two novels and one poem, in Spanish.
  **Model answer:** Novels: *Noli Me TГЎngere* and *El Filibusterismo*. Poem: *Mi Гәltimo adiГіs*.
- **Q8.3-4 [8.3]** вҖ” What is a "personaje" and how can it relate to the author?
  **Model answer:** A personaje is a character in a story; though fictional, a character such as
  CrisГіstomo Ibarra can reflect the author's own life ("aunqueвҖҰ ficticio, sin embargo refleja
  la vida del propio Rizal").

### Element 8.4

- **Q8.4-1 [8.4]** вҖ” What is the difference between an adjective and an adverb when describing
  food?
  **Model answer:** An adjective describes a noun and agrees with it ("un sabor **agridulce**");
  an adverb modifies a verb/adjective and is invariable, often ending in -mente
  ("**deliciosamente** variada").
- **Q8.4-2 [8.4]** вҖ” Describe the texture of lechГіn using two adjectives.
  **Model answer:** "Crujiente por fuera y jugoso por dentro."
- **Q8.4-3 [8.4]** вҖ” Say the Spanish for: flavor, dish, recipe, sweet.
  **Model answer:** sabor, platillo, receta, dulce.

### Element 8.5

- **Q8.5-1 [8.5]** вҖ” When should you address someone with "usted" rather than "tГә"?
  **Model answer:** With strangers, elders, superiors, or in formal/respectful contexts вҖ” as a
  matter of courtesy ("por cortesГӯa, conviene tratarlo de usted").
- **Q8.5-2 [8.5]** вҖ” How does the verb agree with "usted"? Give an example.
  **Model answer:** "Usted" takes third-person singular agreement: "Le pido que se
  **siente**, don Manuel"; "Reciba **usted** mi cordial saludo".
- **Q8.5-3 [8.5]** вҖ” What titles show respect when greeting someone formally?
  **Model answer:** don/doГұa + first name, seГұor/seГұora + surname, professional titles
  (seГұora directora). Pair with a courteous greeting (saludo cordial).
- **Q8.5-4 [8.5]** вҖ” What does a good "anfitriГіn" do, and how do you say it courteously?
  **Model answer:** A host makes guests feel welcome: "Como buen anfitriГіn, nos pidiГі que
  nos **sintiГ©ramos** como en casa."

### Element 8.6

- **Q8.6-1 [8.6]** вҖ” How do you contrast past and present when discussing folklore?
  **Model answer:** Use time markers with tense contrast: "**Antes** los mitos **explicaban**
  la naturaleza; **ahora** la ciencia **ofrece** otras respuestas."
- **Q8.6-2 [8.6]** вҖ” What is the difference between leyenda, mito and cuento?
  **Model answer:** Leyenda = a legend (semi-historical, place/people based); mito = a myth
  (explains nature/origins); cuento = a tale/story (often for entertainment, e.g. duendes).
- **Q8.6-3 [8.6]** вҖ” What is "tradiciГіn oral" and why does it matter?
  **Model answer:** Oral tradition is culture passed by word of mouth across generations; it
  transmitted a people's history and "todavГӯa hoy mantiene viva su memoria."

## Critical Aspects of Competency

To be judged **competent** in Module 8, the learner must demonstrate ALL of the following:

- Sustains discussion of culture, traditions and the arts in Spanish across **at least four** of
  the six elements, including the Rizal anchor (8.3).
- Controls the **present perfect vs. preterite** contrast when talking about festivities (8.1).
- Produces accurate **comparatives and superlatives** to evaluate cultural phenomena (8.1).
- Uses the **present subjunctive** correctly to express opinion, doubt and recommendation
  about the arts (8.2).
- Narrates a cultural/literary account with **narrative connectors and past-tense contrast**
  (8.3).
- Maintains appropriate **register and courtesy (usted / formal forms of address)** in a social
  protocol exchange (8.5).
- Handles **present/past contrast** when retelling folklore (8.6).
- Uses module vocabulary (Cinco RaГӯces roots) accurately and in context, not as isolated
  recall.
- Communicates comprehensibly at **CEFR B1вҖ“B2**: coherent, connected discourse with
  self-correction.

## Methods of Assessment

Competence is assessed through a combination of:

- **Demonstration / observation** вҖ” the learner performs the can-do tasks in the
  Demonstration Checklist (oral role-plays, narrations, comparisons, opinions); rater records
  S / NY against observable criteria.
- **Oral questioning** вҖ” underpinning-knowledge questions from the Question Bank, tagged by
  element, to confirm the learner understands the why behind the performance.
- **Portfolio of Cinco RaГӯces "can-do" summaries** вҖ” the learner submits short self-authored
  summaries built on the module's root lemmas (festividad, arte, literatura, gastronomГӯa,
  costumbre, folclore families), evidencing vocabulary in productive use.
- **Written task** вҖ” a short narrative/opinion piece (e.g. a paragraph on Rizal for 8.3, a
  festival comparison for 8.1, or a legend retelling for 8.6) marked against the relevant
  performance criteria and key grammar.

Evidence from element 8.3 additionally emits toward the cross-cutting **Rizal Studies вҖ”
Completion Record**.
