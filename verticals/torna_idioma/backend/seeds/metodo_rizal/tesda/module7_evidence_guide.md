# Evidence Guide — Module 7: ¿A qué te dedicas? (What Do You Do for a Living?)

## Unit of Competency
Communicate in work and professional contexts in Spanish. (CEFR B1)

The learner can describe their own and others' occupations, talk about professional
skills and qualities, navigate a job interview and present a CV, discuss the work
environment and hypothetical work situations, use common office expressions and
prepositions of place, and narrate their own work experience using past tenses.

## Elements & Performance Criteria

### Element 7.1 — Work and professions (ser / hacer)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Asks and answers "¿A qué te dedicas?" and states own occupation using *ser* + profession (e.g. "Soy enfermera").
  2. Describes what someone does day-to-day using *hacer* (e.g. "Hace planos para edificios").
  3. Names at least five professions with correct gender agreement (ingeniero/ingeniera, médico/médica).
  4. Distinguishes *ser* (identity/profession) from *hacer* (activity/task) without prompting.
  5. Describes a worker's character using profession-derived adjectives (e.g. "trabajador").
- **Linked roots:** m7-dedicarse, m7-profesion, m7-empleo, m7-ingeniero, m7-trabajador, m7-médico, m7-maestro, m7-abogado, m7-comerciante

### Element 7.2 — Skills and qualities (qualifying / possessive adjectives)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Lists own professional skills using possessive adjectives ("Mi mayor habilidad es…").
  2. Describes a colleague or boss with qualifying adjectives in correct gender/number agreement ("una empleada capaz").
  3. Uses at least four work-quality adjectives appropriately (responsable, capaz, puntual, organizado).
  4. Forms possessive phrases across persons (mi / tu / su / nuestro) referring to people and teams at work.
  5. Combines noun + qualifying adjective in attributive position with agreement ("trabajadores dedicados").
- **Linked roots:** m7-habilidad, m7-responsable, m7-capaz, m7-puntual, m7-organizado

### Element 7.3 — Interviews and CV (past tenses for experience)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Narrates a past interview using preterite ("Ayer tuve una entrevista").
  2. States prior duties/conditions using imperfect ("Hablaba español e inglés").
  3. Describes the CV/application process using *currículum*, *solicitar*, *solicitud*.
  4. Explains why a company hired them, combining preterite and imperfect ("Me contrató porque hablaba…").
  5. Quantifies accumulated experience in years/months with correct past tense ("Tenía cinco años de experiencia").
- **Linked roots:** m7-entrevista, m7-currículum, m7-contratar, m7-solicitar, m7-experiencia

### Element 7.4 — The work environment (subjunctive for hypotheticals)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Expresses a workplace wish with *ojalá que* + present subjunctive ("Ojalá que el jefe sea justo").
  2. Describes a desired team/environment with a subjunctive relative clause ("un equipo que colabore").
  3. States a condition using *siempre que* / *a menos que* + subjunctive ("siempre que el sueldo sea suficiente").
  4. Expresses doubt about a work outcome with *dudar que* / *espero que* + subjunctive.
  5. Selects subjunctive vs. indicative correctly depending on certainty/hypothesis.
- **Linked roots:** m7-jefe, m7-equipo, m7-ambiente, m7-sueldo, m7-ascender

### Element 7.5 — Office expressions (prepositions of place / relationship)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Locates objects/people in the office using prepositions of place (al lado de, junto a, debajo de, sobre).
  2. States the time and place of a meeting ("La reunión es a las tres, en la sala del tercer piso").
  3. Reports a workplace action using common office nouns (informe, correo, reunión).
  4. Describes who they communicated with using *con* + colleague/department ("Hablé con mi colega").
  5. Uses at least four place prepositions accurately in connected speech.
- **Linked roots:** m7-oficina, m7-reunión, m7-colega, m7-informe, m7-correo

### Element 7.6 — Work experience (preterite / imperfect / present perfect)
- **Performance criteria**
  1. Narrates the start of a job using preterite + imperfect background ("Empecé… cuando estudiaba").
  2. Explains a resignation with cause, mixing preterite and imperfect ("Renuncié porque ganaba poco").
  3. Reports recent/ongoing achievements with present perfect ("He aprendido mucho", "He logrado mis metas").
  4. Narrates a completed career event (retirement, founding a company) with preterite ("Se jubiló a los sesenta").
  5. Contrasts the three past forms correctly in a single connected account of a career path.
- **Linked roots:** m7-empezar, m7-renunciar, m7-aprender, m7-lograr, m7-jubilarse, m7-fundar

## Oral / Written Question Bank

### Element 7.1 (ser / hacer)
1. **[7.1]** Q: When do you use *ser* and when do you use *hacer* to talk about jobs?
   A: *Ser* + noun states identity/profession ("Soy ingeniero"); *hacer* describes the activity/task done ("Hace planos"). Profession = ser; daily action = hacer.
2. **[7.1]** Q: Give the masculine and feminine forms of "engineer" and "doctor."
   A: ingeniero/ingeniera; médico/médica.
3. **[7.1]** Q: Translate "What do you do for a living? I work in teaching."
   A: "¿A qué te dedicas? Me dedico a la enseñanza."
4. **[7.1]** Q: How is "trabajador" both a noun and an adjective?
   A: As a noun it means "worker"; as an adjective it means "hard-working" ("Es muy trabajador").

### Element 7.2 (qualifying / possessive adjectives)
1. **[7.2]** Q: How do possessive adjectives agree — with the owner or the thing owned?
   A: With the thing owned in number (and *nuestro* also in gender): "mi habilidad", "mis habilidades", "nuestra jefa".
2. **[7.2]** Q: Make "capaz" agree with a plural feminine noun.
   A: "empleadas capaces" (capaz → capaces in plural; no gender change).
3. **[7.2]** Q: Name three positive work qualities in Spanish.
   A: responsable, puntual, organizado/a (also capaz).
4. **[7.2]** Q: Where does the qualifying adjective normally go relative to the noun?
   A: Usually after the noun ("una empleada capaz"), agreeing in gender and number.

### Element 7.3 (past tenses for experience)
1. **[7.3]** Q: Why "Me contrató porque hablaba español" and not "habló"?
   A: *Contrató* (preterite) is the completed event; *hablaba* (imperfect) is the ongoing background state/ability at that time.
2. **[7.3]** Q: What's the Spanish for "CV / résumé" and "to apply for a job"?
   A: "currículum" (also "hoja de vida"); "solicitar (un puesto)".
3. **[7.3]** Q: Put in the past: "I have five years of experience" → describing the moment of promotion.
   A: "Tenía cinco años de experiencia cuando me ascendieron."
4. **[7.3]** Q: Which past tense narrates the single events of an interview?
   A: The preterite ("tuve una entrevista", "me hizo preguntas").

### Element 7.4 (subjunctive for hypotheticals)
1. **[7.4]** Q: After *ojalá que*, which mood do you use? Give an example.
   A: The subjunctive: "Ojalá que el jefe sea justo."
2. **[7.4]** Q: Why "un equipo que colabore" and not "que colabora"?
   A: The team is hypothetical/desired (not yet identified), so the relative clause takes the subjunctive.
3. **[7.4]** Q: Complete with the correct mood: "Aceptaría el puesto siempre que el sueldo ____ (ser) suficiente."
   A: "sea" — *siempre que* expressing a condition triggers the subjunctive.
4. **[7.4]** Q: When do you use indicative instead in a work sentence?
   A: When the fact is certain/real ("Sé que el sueldo es bueno") rather than wished/doubted/hypothetical.

### Element 7.5 (prepositions of place / relationship)
1. **[7.5]** Q: Give three prepositions of place used to locate office objects.
   A: al lado de / junto a, debajo de, sobre (also "en", "delante de").
2. **[7.5]** Q: How do you say "The meeting is at three, in the third-floor room"?
   A: "La reunión es a las tres, en la sala del tercer piso." (*a* for time, *en* for place).
3. **[7.5]** Q: Which preposition marks who you spoke with at work?
   A: *con* — "Hablé con mi colega."
4. **[7.5]** Q: Translate "I put the report on the table, under the other documents."
   A: "Puse el informe sobre la mesa, debajo de los demás documentos."

### Element 7.6 (preterite / imperfect / present perfect)
1. **[7.6]** Q: When do you choose present perfect over preterite for work experience?
   A: Present perfect (*he aprendido*) for recent/ongoing experience still connected to now; preterite (*aprendí*) for finished, time-bounded events.
2. **[7.6]** Q: Explain the tenses in "Empecé a trabajar cuando estudiaba."
   A: *Empecé* (preterite) = completed start event; *estudiaba* (imperfect) = the ongoing background situation.
3. **[7.6]** Q: Put in present perfect: "achieve all my professional goals this year."
   A: "He logrado todas mis metas profesionales este año."
4. **[7.6]** Q: Which tense narrates "He retired at sixty" and why?
   A: Preterite ("Se jubiló a los sesenta") — a single completed past event.

## Critical Aspects of Competency
- Correctly distinguishes **ser vs. hacer** when stating professions vs. describing job activities.
- Applies **gender/number agreement** consistently to profession nouns, qualifying adjectives, and possessive adjectives.
- Selects the correct **past tense** (preterite vs. imperfect vs. present perfect) for the communicative purpose when narrating work experience and interviews.
- Uses the **subjunctive** accurately in workplace wishes, conditions, doubts, and hypothetical relative clauses, and recognizes when indicative is required.
- Uses **prepositions of place and relationship** correctly to locate people/objects and to schedule and report office interactions.
- Sustains a coherent, intelligible exchange about own occupation, skills, and career path using Module 7 Cinco Raíces vocabulary.

---

## Methods of Assessment
- **Demonstration / observation:** learner performs the can-do tasks in the checklist above (role-play interview, describe own job and a colleague, locate office items, narrate career path); rater marks S / NY against observable criteria.
- **Oral questioning:** underpinning-knowledge questions from the bank, tagged by element, confirm the grammar reasoning behind the performance (ser/hacer, agreement, past-tense choice, subjunctive triggers, prepositions).
- **Portfolio:** the learner's **Cinco Raíces "can-do" summaries** for each of the seven Module 7 sessions (35 roots), showing self-rated mastery with example sentences.
- **Written task:** a short CV-style profile plus a paragraph narrating one work experience (mixing preterite/imperfect/present perfect) and one paragraph of workplace wishes (subjunctive).

---

## Context of Assessment
- Conducted in a classroom or supervised online session, individually and/or in pairs for the interview role-play.
- Permitted tools: profession picture cards, a blank CV template, an office floor-plan diagram for the prepositions task; no full-sentence scripts or translation apps during performance.
- Working/instruction language of the guide and oral questioning: **English** (with **Tagalog** support permitted — Tagalog scaffolding wording is `[FIL — pending G3 review]`).
- **Spanish is required for all target performance** (the can-do demonstrations and written task must be produced in Spanish at CEFR B1).
