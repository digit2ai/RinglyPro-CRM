# Evidence Guide — Module 11: Environment and Sustainability

## Unit of Competency
Discuss environment and sustainability in Spanish — express wishes and hopes about environmental outcomes, reason about hypothetical scenarios, describe problems and solutions, and argue for individual and collective responsibility using accurate, connected discourse. (CEFR B2)

**Key grammar for this unit:** present subjunctive for wishes/hopes (*ojalá que*, *espero que*, *quiero que*); conditional + imperfect subjunctive for hypotheticals (*si tuviéramos…, encontraríamos…*); indefinite pronouns and quantifiers (*alguien, nadie, cualquiera, ningún, pocos, demasiado*); relative pronouns (*que, quien, cuyo/cuya*); modal verbs of obligation and possibility (*deber, tener que, poder, debería*).

---

## Elements & Performance Criteria

### Element 11.1 — Environment & awareness
- **Performance criteria** (observable can-do statements):
  1. States hopes and wishes about caring for the environment using the present subjunctive after *ojalá que / espero que / quiero que* (e.g., "Ojalá que cuidemos el medio ambiente").
  2. Names core concepts — *medio ambiente, naturaleza, sostenibilidad* — and uses their derived forms (*ambiental, natural, sostenible*) in context.
  3. Explains why awareness-raising (*concienciar*) matters, addressed to a stated audience (young people, a community).
  4. Expresses that something is important using *es importante que* + subjunctive (e.g., "Es importante que protejamos los arrecifes").
- **Linked roots:** m11-medioambiente, m11-concienciar, m11-naturaleza, m11-proteger, m11-sostenible

### Element 11.2 — Environmental problems & solutions
- **Performance criteria:**
  1. Describes an environmental problem (pollution, deforestation, global warming) naming cause and effect.
  2. Constructs hypothetical statements with *si* + imperfect subjunctive + conditional (e.g., "Si redujéramos las emisiones, el calentamiento global avanzaría más despacio").
  3. Proposes at least one realistic solution (*solución, mitigar*) for a named local problem (Pasig River, Luzon floods, garbage).
  4. Uses *sin* + noun to state a counterfactual condition ("Sin tanta deforestación, habría menos inundaciones").
- **Linked roots:** m11-contaminar, m11-deforestacion, m11-solucion, m11-calentamiento, m11-mitigar

### Element 11.3 — Recycling & waste
- **Performance criteria:**
  1. Describes recycling and waste-handling habits using indefinite pronouns and quantifiers (*algunos, muchos, pocos, ningún, cualquiera, demasiado*).
  2. Reports what others do or fail to do (e.g., "Algunos vecinos reciclan, pero muchos todavía no separan nada").
  3. Makes a recommendation about single-use plastic using *deber/debería* ("Ningún mercado debería usar tanto plástico").
  4. Explains the reuse–recycle distinction (*reutilizar* vs *reciclar*) with everyday examples.
- **Linked roots:** m11-reciclar, m11-residuo, m11-plastico, m11-reutilizar, m11-basura

### Element 11.4 — Renewable energy
- **Performance criteria:**
  1. Distinguishes energy sources — fossil (*combustible*) vs renewable (*renovable, solar, eólico*) — and states why renewables matter.
  2. Adds defining information with relative pronouns *que* and *cuyo/cuya* (e.g., "el parque eólico, cuyas turbinas son enormes").
  3. Describes a concrete renewable installation and its benefit (solar panel on the roof, Ilocos Norte wind farm).
  4. Explains, with a relative clause, why fossil fuels are not renewable ("los combustibles fósiles, que tardan millones de años en formarse").
- **Linked roots:** m11-energia, m11-renovable, m11-solar, m11-eolico, m11-combustible

### Element 11.5 — Ecotourism
- **Performance criteria:**
  1. Explains how ecotourism can protect natural sites, using conditional clauses with *si* + present (e.g., "El ecoturismo puede proteger las islas si los visitantes respetan las normas").
  2. States obligations and prohibitions for visitors using modal verbs and *no se puede / deben* ("No se puede pescar en la reserva sin permiso").
  3. Describes biodiversity and conservation (*biodiversidad, conservar, reserva*) at a named Philippine site (Bohol, Palawan).
  4. Gives a rule or instruction for sustainable visiting (follow the marked *sendero*) with a purpose clause (*para no…*).
- **Linked roots:** m11-ecoturismo, m11-conservar, m11-biodiversidad, m11-reserva, m11-sendero

### Element 11.6 — Individual & collective responsibility (modal verbs)
- **Performance criteria:**
  1. Recommends individual actions using *deber / debería / poder / tener que* (e.g., "Cada persona debería ahorrar agua").
  2. Expresses possibility and obligation for collective actions (*podemos reducir…, las comunidades tienen que colaborar*).
  3. States a personal commitment (*comprometerse*) to sustainable living using a modal + infinitive.
  4. Names a duty of government or citizens (*fomentar, denunciar, responsabilidad*) and justifies it.
  5. Connects individual and collective levels in a short argued statement (small daily actions → shared responsibility).
- **Linked roots:** m11-ahorrar, m11-reducir, m11-consumir, m11-evitar, m11-cuidar, m11-responsabilidad, m11-colaborar, m11-denunciar, m11-fomentar, m11-comprometerse

## Oral / Written Question Bank
Underpinning-knowledge questions with model answers. Each is tagged with its element code.

### Element 11.1
1. **[11.1]** When do you use the present subjunctive after *ojalá que* and *espero que*?
   *Model:* After expressions of wish/hope, the verb in the dependent clause takes the present subjunctive because the outcome is desired, not asserted as fact — e.g., "Ojalá que **cuidemos** el medio ambiente," "Espero que la escuela **conciencie** a los jóvenes."
2. **[11.1]** Give a derived adjective from *medio ambiente* and use it in a phrase.
   *Model:* *ambiental* — "la protección **ambiental**."
3. **[11.1]** What structure expresses importance, and what mood follows it?
   *Model:* *Es importante que* + present subjunctive — "Es importante que **protejamos** los arrecifes de coral."

### Element 11.2
1. **[11.2]** How do you form a hypothetical "if" sentence about an unlikely situation?
   *Model:* *Si* + imperfect subjunctive in the condition, conditional in the result — "Si **redujéramos** las emisiones, el calentamiento **avanzaría** más despacio."
2. **[11.2]** What does *mitigar* mean and how is it different from *solucionar*?
   *Model:* *mitigar* = to ease/lessen the effects; *solucionar* = to resolve the problem itself. You mitigate the effects of climate change; you solve the garbage problem.
3. **[11.2]** Rewrite "Si no hubiera deforestación…" using *sin*.
   *Model:* "**Sin** tanta deforestación, habría menos inundaciones en Luzón."

### Element 11.3
1. **[11.3]** What is the difference between *algunos*, *muchos*, and *ningún*?
   *Model:* They are quantifiers of degree: *algunos* = some, *muchos* = many, *ningún* = no/none (used before a masculine singular noun: *ningún mercado*). They must agree with the noun.
2. **[11.3]** Use *cualquiera* in a sentence about reuse.
   *Model:* "**Cualquiera** puede reutilizar las botellas en vez de tirarlas." (*cualquiera* = anyone.)
3. **[11.3]** When is *demasiado* an adjective vs an adverb?
   *Model:* As an adjective it agrees with the noun (*demasiados residuos*); as an adverb modifying a verb/adjective it is invariable (*producen demasiado*).

### Element 11.4
1. **[11.4]** What is the function of the relative pronoun *cuyo/cuya*?
   *Model:* It expresses possession ("whose") and agrees with the thing possessed, not the owner — "el parque eólico, **cuyas** turbinas son enormes."
2. **[11.4]** Why are fossil fuels not renewable? Answer with a *que*-clause.
   *Model:* "Los combustibles fósiles, **que** tardan millones de años en formarse, no son renovables."
3. **[11.4]** Name two renewable sources and one non-renewable source.
   *Model:* Renewable: *energía solar*, *energía eólica*. Non-renewable: *combustibles fósiles / carbón*.

### Element 11.5
1. **[11.5]** How can you state a condition that is realistic (likely true)?
   *Model:* *Si* + present indicative + present/future — "El ecoturismo puede proteger las islas **si** los visitantes **respetan** las normas."
2. **[11.5]** How do you express a prohibition impersonally?
   *Model:* *No se puede* + infinitive — "**No se puede** pescar en la reserva sin permiso."
3. **[11.5]** What is *biodiversidad*, and give one way tourists can protect it?
   *Model:* The variety of living things in a habitat; tourists protect it by following the marked *sendero* and not stepping on plants.

### Element 11.6
1. **[11.6]** Compare *deber*, *tener que*, and *debería*. What does each express?
   *Model:* *deber* + infinitive = obligation/duty; *tener que* + infinitive = strong necessity; *debería* (conditional) = softened advice/recommendation — "Cada persona **debería** ahorrar agua."
2. **[11.6]** How do you express possibility with a modal verb?
   *Model:* *poder* + infinitive — "**Podemos** reducir nuestra huella de carbono si caminamos más."
3. **[11.6]** How is *comprometerse* used to state a commitment?
   *Model:* *comprometerse a* + infinitive — "Cada uno de nosotros debería **comprometerse a** vivir de forma más sostenible."
4. **[11.6]** Give one duty of government and one of citizens using a modal.
   *Model:* "El gobierno **debe fomentar** el transporte público"; "Los ciudadanos **pueden denunciar** a quienes tiran basura."

## Critical Aspects of Competency
The learner must demonstrate ALL of the following for overall competence in Module 11:
- Forms and uses the **present subjunctive** correctly after wish/hope/importance expressions (*ojalá que, espero que, es importante que*).
- Constructs at least one well-formed **hypothetical** with *si* + imperfect subjunctive + conditional, and one realistic *si* + indicative condition.
- Uses **indefinite pronouns and quantifiers** (*alguien/nadie/cualquiera/ningún/pocos/demasiado*) with correct agreement.
- Uses **relative pronouns** *que* and *cuyo/cuya* to add defining information, with correct agreement on *cuyo/cuya*.
- Uses **modal verbs** (*deber, tener que, poder, debería*) to express obligation, necessity, possibility, and recommendation across individual and collective levels.
- Deploys environment-and-sustainability vocabulary from the six elements accurately and in context (problems, solutions, energy, ecotourism, actions).
- Sustains connected, comprehensible B2-level discourse on an environmental topic, not isolated memorized phrases.

---

## Methods of Assessment
Competence is assessed through a combination of:
- **Direct demonstration / observation** — learner performs the can-do tasks in the Demonstration Checklist (spoken roleplay, short oral presentation on a local environmental issue, simulated visitor-rules briefing for a reserve).
- **Oral questioning** — rater poses underpinning-knowledge questions from the Question Bank to confirm understanding of the key grammar (subjunctive, conditionals, quantifiers, relatives, modals).
- **Portfolio of "Cinco Raíces" can-do summaries** — learner's collected session records evidencing the 35 roots across the six elements, with self-authored example sentences.
- **Written task** — a short paragraph (B2) proposing solutions to a Philippine environmental problem, required to include at least one subjunctive wish, one hypothetical, one relative clause, and two modal recommendations.

---

## Context of Assessment
- Conducted in a **classroom or supervised online** environment.
- **Tools allowed:** Cinco Raíces vocabulary cards/portfolio for reference during preparation; no machine translation during the assessed performance.
- **Language of instruction:** English, with Tagalog scaffolding permitted for clarification. `[FIL — pending G3 review]` Tagalog learner-facing instructions and clarifications are to be supplied and review-gated before use.
- **Target performance language:** Spanish is required for all assessed can-do tasks and the written task; underpinning-knowledge answers may be given in Spanish or English.
- Assessment is conducted by a qualified assessor; performance is judged Satisfactory / Not Yet against the criteria above.
